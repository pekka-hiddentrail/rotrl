# Session 1 — The Swallowtail Festival

*Sandpoint, Varisia — 1st of Lamashan, 4707 AR*

---

The road from Magnimar runs north along the cliff edge for the better part of a day before Sandpoint appears — a scattering of shingled rooftops and white-painted walls above a crescent harbour, the sea hammering the rocks fifty feet below. It is smaller than you might have expected. It is also, today, louder than it has any right to be.

The **Swallowtail Festival** has drawn people from every farm and fishing village within two days' travel. The market square is packed. Garlands of late-season wildflowers drape the storefronts. Children are everywhere — chasing each other between the legs of adults, clutching honeycakes, releasing the paper swallowtail butterflies sold three for a copper at the stalls near the north gate. The smell in the air is woodsmoke, frying oil, salt wind off the gulf, and something faintly resinous from the new timber of the cathedral.

The **Cathedral of Desna** stands at the far end of the square, and it is impossible to miss. Five years ago the old one burned — the whole town remembers it, and the whole town has spent five years rebuilding what was lost. The new building is clean stone and fresh timber, its windows not yet filled with proper glass, its dedication postponed until this morning. Father Abstalar Zantus has been conducting prayer services since dawn. The consecration — the moment the cathedral is formally given to Desna — is expected around midday, after the Mayor's welcome and the release of the live swallowtails kept in the wicker cages stacked near the steps.

Mayor **Kendra Deverin** is visible near the cathedral steps, speaking with a small group of civic worthies — a brisk, composed woman in her early forties who looks entirely at ease with a crowd. Sheriff **Belor Hemlock** stands a little apart, scanning the square with the patient expression of a man who has been watching festivals long enough to know exactly which part of the afternoon usually causes trouble.

The locals are in genuine good spirits. There is free cider from the barrels the miller rolled out at sunrise. Somebody has a fiddle. Somebody else, near the harbour gate, is losing a bet involving an eel.

You arrived this morning — each of you from wherever you came from, for whatever reason brought you to a small coastal town on a consecration day. Nobody has asked you why you are here. Nobody needs to. Half of Varisia seems to have had the same idea.

The speeches will begin soon. For now, the square belongs to everyone.
