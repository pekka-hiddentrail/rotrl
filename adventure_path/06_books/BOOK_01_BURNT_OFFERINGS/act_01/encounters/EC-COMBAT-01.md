# EC-COM-01 — Wave 1: Initial Goblin Assault

**Scene:** S4 — Goblin Assault: Festival Square  
**System:** Pathfinder 1e  
**CR:** 3 (4–6 warriors CR 1/3 each + 1 warchanter CR 1)  
**XP:** ~810–1,335 depending on warrior count  

---

## Setup

The alarm sounds mid-ceremony. Goblins pour into the square from the north. The warchanter positions herself atop a crate or market stall 30–40 ft from the nearest PCs and begins singing immediately. Warriors fan out, attacking festival-goers and setting fire to stalls.

The PCs are not surprised — the alarm bell gives them a moment to react.

---

## Combatants

### Goblin Warriors (4–6)
CR 1/3 | AC 16 | HP 5 | Init +6  
Melee: dogslicer +2 (1d4/19–20)  
Ranged: shortbow +4 (1d4/×3)  
Fort +3 | Ref +2 | Will −1  
**Morale:** Flee if 3+ warriors fall, or if warchanter is silenced.

### Goblin Warchanter (1)
CR 1 | AC 14 | HP 8 | Init +3  
Melee: whip +2 (1d3 nonlethal), dogslicer +1 (1d4/19–20)  
Ranged: shortbow +5 (1d4+1/×3)  
Bardic Music 4/day — Inspire Courage +1 (attack and damage to all active goblins while singing)  
**Tactics:** Stays out of melee. Sings constantly. If threatened in melee, falls back but does not flee until morale threshold.  
**Priority:** Silencing her ends Wave 1 morale immediately.

---

## Tactical Notes

- Warchanter's Inspire Courage means warriors hit at +3 / damage +1 while she sings
- Warriors prioritise civilians and unguarded stalls over the PCs unless attacked
- Two warriors close on the trapped family at the food cart (see EC-CIV-01)
- Sheriff Hemlock is 60 ft away and closing; he engages warriors, not the warchanter

---

## Wave End Condition

Warchanter killed or flees → warriors' morale check (Will DC 10 or flee). Wave 2 follows immediately.

---

## Loot (per warrior)
Dogslicer (1 gp), shortbow (30 gp), 20 arrows, leather armour (10 gp)  
Warchanter: whip, dogslicer, shortbow, potion of cure light wounds, 20 gp
