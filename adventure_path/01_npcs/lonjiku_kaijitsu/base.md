# Lonjiku Kaijitsu

**Aliases:** lonjiku, kaijitsu, glassworks, glassworks owner, lonjiku kaijitsu
**Locations:** glassworks, sandpoint glassworks

## Personality

Gruff, businesslike, and emotionally closed off. Lonjiku built the Glassworks into a successful operation through discipline and demand — the same approach he applied to his family, with worse results. He is not cruel in a theatrical sense and not unintelligent, but he is simply indifferent to feelings, including his own, and has no patience for anyone who cannot keep up.

## Appearance

A severe Tian man in his late fifties, still upright and sharp-eyed, dressed with the precise formality of someone who considers appearance a professional obligation. His expression gives nothing away and he offers nothing voluntarily.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Festival day:** Not present. He dislikes celebrations and does not attend
- **During/after goblin attack:** At the Glassworks; may be seen dismissing the commotion
- **General routine:** At the Glassworks from early morning through evening; direct contact requires going there
- **Availability:** Low; he will not seek out the PCs

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Unfriendly — not actively hostile but has no interest in the PCs unless they affect his business
- **Warms to PCs who:** Address him on business terms; demonstrate competence or authority; show no interest in his personal affairs
- **Cools toward PCs who:** Pry into family matters; appear to be allied with Ameiko in any domestic dispute; make emotional appeals
- **Voice/mannerism:** Clipped and transactional; does not ask follow-up questions; ends conversations when he is done, not when the other person is
- **Emphasise:** The contrast between business competence and personal failure; the way he speaks about Tsuto — or refuses to
- **Avoid:** Giving him warmth he has not earned; making him theatrical

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy:** DC 16 — he has little interest in goodwill; success gets professional courtesy, not friendliness
- **Bluff:** DC 16 — he is cynical and hard to impress; success when Tsuto is mentioned directly — unlocks: Tsuto's parentage (see Secrets)
- **Intimidate:** DC 14 — business threats land harder than personal ones; success under specific threat to the business — unlocks: Financial irregularities (see Secrets)
- **Intimidate (general):** DC 12 — he takes business threats seriously and will respond to protect his interests

## Secrets

- **Tsuto's parentage:** Tsuto is not his biological son — he is the product of an affair Lonjiku's wife had. Lonjiku knows and has punished Tsuto for it his entire life through coldness and exclusion. He has never spoken of it. — *Unlocked by: Bluff DC 16 or Intimidate DC 14 when Tsuto is mentioned directly*
- **Financial irregularities:** The Glassworks has been used for moving goods that don't appear on the official ledger. Lonjiku won't say for whom or why. — *Unlocked by: Intimidate DC 14 (cross-reference) under specific threat to the business*

## State Handling

- **If Killed:** Economic recovery in Sandpoint slows; Ameiko's emotional arc shifts significantly
- **If Cooperative (reluctantly):** Provides Glassworks access and limited local business intelligence

## Narrative Function

Demonstrates the mundane cruelty behind Sandpoint's prosperous surface. His fate — and what Tsuto does to him — reveals that the goblin threat is not external. It came from inside the family.
