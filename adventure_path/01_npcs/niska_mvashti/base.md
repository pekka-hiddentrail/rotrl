# Niska Mvashti

**Aliases:** niska, mvashti, madame mvashti, seer, varisian elder, the elder
**Locations:** mvashti manor, manor, area 28, countryside

## Personality

Ancient, mysterious, and possessed of a deep brooding worry about the current age's departure from prophecy — Madame Mvashti has watched Sandpoint for its entire forty-year history and carries the weight of what she has seen. She is not unkind but she is unsentimental, and she gives her predictions rarely and without softening. She despises the Sczarni as a corruption of Varisian tradition and is one of the oldest and most powerful individuals in Sandpoint, though she rarely makes this apparent. Her prophecies have been failing recently, and the events now unfolding feel like a convergence she has feared for decades.

## Appearance

Mvashti is an old woman in the way that very old trees are old — the age is visible everywhere but it does not suggest fragility. Her face is deeply lined, her eyes are dark and watchful, and she moves with the deliberate economy of someone who has long since stopped wasting motion on anything unnecessary. She wears Varisian traveling clothes even when seated at home, as though she is always either arriving from somewhere or about to leave for it.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **At the festival:** Present but seated at the edge of the crowd. Watching rather than participating. Local Varisians cluster respectfully nearby.
- **After the attack:** At her manor (area 28). Access requires effort — local Varisians act as informal gatekeepers.
- **General:** Her manor most days. Takes long walks in the countryside accompanied by druids from the hinterlands.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Indifferent. She takes the measure of everyone slowly and does not extend trust on first meeting.
- **What warms her:** Genuine engagement rather than flattery; respect for Varisian tradition; demonstrating real knowledge of Thassilonian history; following through on what one says.
- **What cools her:** Any association with or excusing of the Sczarni; disrespect for Varisian tradition; superficial curiosity without real intent.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Speaks in measured, unhurried sentences. Does not repeat herself. When she is done with a conversation, she is done. Silences are comfortable for her; let them breathe.
- **Emphasise:** The weight of her age and knowledge. The fact that her prophecies are failing is genuinely frightening to her, though she will not show fear — only increased gravity. Her offer of a card reading is a significant gesture of regard.
- **Avoid:** Making her feel like a cryptic-hint dispenser. She is a person, not a plot kiosk. Her information comes with her trust; her trust is earned.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (respectful approach):** DC 15 — acknowledges the PCs as worth speaking with and offers a brief reading — unlocks: Failing prophecies (see Secrets)
- **Knowledge (local / Varisian):** DC 14 — understanding Varisian tradition impresses her and improves attitude by one step
- **Knowledge (history) re: Thassilon / Lost Coast:** DC 16 — engaging her on the true ancient history earns real interest; DC 16 combined with Diplomacy DC 18 both met — unlocks: Old Light knowledge (see Secrets)
- **Sense Motive:** DC 18 — she is deeply worried; her prophecies have been failing and the current events feel like a feared convergence
- **Spellcraft:** DC 15 — PCs notice her aura carries unusually layered divine and arcane magic; she is more powerful than she appears
- **Diplomacy (after full trust established):** DC 20 — unlocks: Sczarni origins (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Failing prophecies:** Her harrow readings and bone castings have been yielding contradictory or null results for months. She believes the events now unfolding exceed the scope of normal divination — something is actively disrupting the threads of fate in this region. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 15 (see Social Checks) after a reading*
- **Old Light knowledge:** She knows the Old Light was not a lighthouse. She has kept this to herself because the truth — a Thassilonian weapon of mass destruction — would cause more harm than silence. She will share it only with someone she is certain can handle the knowledge responsibly. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 18 + Knowledge (history) DC 16 both met*
- **Sczarni origins:** She knows the specific names and histories of the Sandpoint Sczarni leadership and has documented evidence of crimes she has never brought to Hemlock — because she has been waiting for the right moment, which she believes is now approaching. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 20 after full trust established*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Offers divination assistance (harrow card readings, bone castings). Provides cryptic but accurate foreshadowing of coming events. Her Varisian community connections provide safe houses and intelligence throughout the region.
- **If Neutral:** Acknowledges PCs politely. Will not offer her gifts unless asked sincerely.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs ally with or excuse the Sczarni, or disrespect Varisian tradition. Refuses any interaction. Her community withdraws cooperation entirely.
- **If Killed:** The oldest living link to Sandpoint's Varisian past is gone. A profound cultural and spiritual loss. Prophecy support and divination resources are lost for the campaign. Druids from the hinterlands may turn hostile if they believe PCs were responsible.

## Narrative Function

Provides the deep-time perspective — she has watched Sandpoint's entire history and carries foreknowledge of the larger Thassilonian convergence. Her failing prophecies signal that what is unfolding now exceeds normal historical patterns. A divination resource for PCs willing to earn her trust, and a mirror that reflects the campaign's larger stakes back at them.
