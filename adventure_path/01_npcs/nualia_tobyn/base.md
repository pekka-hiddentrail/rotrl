# Nualia Tobyn

**Aliases:** nualia, tobyn, nualia tobyn
**Locations:** thistletop, thistletop dungeon

## Personality

Cold, purposeful, and convinced of her own righteousness — Nualia does not see herself as a villain but as someone who survived a life of suffering and chose power over victimhood. She is methodical, patient, and utterly committed to her goal; she does not monologue, she acts. Her grievance against Sandpoint is not entirely fabricated, and that is what makes her dangerous.

## Appearance

A tall woman whose beauty has been reshaped by something older and stranger than she was born with — her left arm has transformed into a clawed, demon-touched limb she does not hide. Her silver hair and pale eyes carry the aasimar heritage Sandpoint once called blessed; what animates them now is something else entirely.

## Location & Availability — Act I

**NOT PRESENT IN ACT I.** Nualia is at Thistletop (Act III). She does not appear in person during the festival or its immediate aftermath. Any information about her comes through investigation and rumour only — old stories about the priest's adopted daughter, whispers about the fire five years ago.

Do not introduce her directly. Do not have NPCs speculate about her unless PCs specifically investigate the cathedral fire.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude at confrontation:** Contemptuous. She has no fear of the PCs and no interest in being understood by them. Dialogue is possible but she controls its terms.
- **Warms (fractionally) when:** PCs demonstrate they know her actual history without flinching from it; when they address her grievance directly rather than her actions; in rare moments, when someone speaks to her without cruelty or condescension — the last person to do that was Father Tobyn.
- **Cools / escalates when:** PCs moralize; when they appeal to Desna or to her old life; when they treat her as a victim rather than as someone who made choices.
- **Voice and mannerism:** Precise and unhurried. She never raises her voice. Questions are rhetorical unless she genuinely wants information. When she decides a conversation is over, it is over.
- **Emphasise:** She is not broken — she is rebuilt. Her conviction is real. The tragedy is that her grievance against Sandpoint is not entirely fabricated.
- **Avoid:** Redemption arcs that feel unearned. If the PCs try to save her, make her make them work for every inch. She does not want to be saved.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy, DC 22 (Thistletop confrontation):** A rare moment of candour — she will confirm the fire and her role in it. — unlocks: **The fire** (see Secrets).
- **Intimidate, DC 18 (Thistletop confrontation):** She discloses the second raid plan under pressure, not out of weakness but because she wants them to understand the scale of what is coming. — unlocks: **Second raid plan** (see Secrets).
- **Direct confrontation with evidence (journal entries):** Unlocks **Lamashtu pact** and **The fire** (see Secrets) without a check.

## Secrets

- **Lamashtu pact:** She made her pact with Lamashtu willingly, in the Catacombs of Wrath beneath Sandpoint, after the fire she herself caused. She felt nothing but relief. She will not deny this if confronted. — *Unlocked by: direct confrontation at Thistletop, or recovered journal entries*
- **The fire:** She set the fire that killed Father Tobyn. It was not an accident and she does not regret it. She was trying to burn away the life Sandpoint had given her. — *Unlocked by: recovered journal, or Diplomacy DC 22 in a moment of rare candour during confrontation*
- **Second raid plan:** The goblin attack was only the first of three planned strikes. The second is intended to be far more destructive. She has the goblin chiefs' commitments but timing depends on completing a ritual at Thistletop. — *Unlocked by: Intimidate DC 18 or Diplomacy DC 22 during Thistletop confrontation*

## State Handling

- **If Redeemed:** Living reminder of mercy and consequence; complicates the moral landscape of later books.
- **If Killed:** Serves as a warning; her story can still be pieced together through investigation.
- **If Escapes:** May reappear as rumour or indirect cult influence in later acts.

**Enforcement:** Nualia must never be treated as disposable. Her arc matters. Do not resolve her offscreen.

## Narrative Function

Prototype of sin-fueled corruption. Her fall predates the goblin raids and was long in coming. She mirrors what the PCs could become if they let resentment override principle. Her arc also reveals the deeper Thassilonian threat beneath the surface of the adventure.
