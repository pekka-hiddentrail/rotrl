# Ameiko Kaijitsu

**Aliases:** ameiko, kaijitsu, innkeeper, rusty dragon, ameiko kaijitsu
**Locations:** rusty dragon, inn, tavern, the rusty dragon, festival square, glassworks

## Personality

Warm, sharp, and quietly restless — Ameiko gave up adventuring but never fully settled into the life of an innkeeper; she still carries herself like someone who expects trouble and knows how to handle it. She left a comfortable noble life to become an adventurer, returned rich and scarred by something she won't discuss, and turned Sandpoint's oldest inn into its most beloved. She is perceptive, funny when relaxed, and guarded about her family — especially her brother Tsuto — burying unresolved pain under competence and humour. She does not pry, but she notices everything, and she plays the shamisen most evenings in the common room.

## Appearance

Ameiko is a Tian woman in her mid-twenties whose athletic build sits oddly with the innkeeper's apron — she moves like someone trained for something more demanding, comfortable in her own body in a way that suggests long practice. Her dark hair is usually pinned back practically, and she has the quick, assessing eyes of someone who spent years reading rooms that might turn dangerous. She is often heard before she is seen: the sound of a shamisen drifting from the common room is as reliable a sign of her presence as anything.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Festival morning:** Running the festival lunch booth — her curry-spiced salmon and winterdrop mead are the clear crowd favorite.
- **During the attack:** Takes cover but keeps her head. May be encountered helping civilians after the fighting.
- **After the attack:** At the Rusty Dragon. She is the link to the Glassworks plot — her disappearance is the trigger for Part Three.
- **Glassworks:** She is abducted by Tsuto and imprisoned in the Glassworks basement. If PCs don't move quickly enough, she is taken to Thistletop.
- **General routine:** Runs the inn from morning through evening; closes after dark.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly and open. Offers food, drink, a room, and rumours freely. Relaxed on first meeting with no preconditions.
- **What warms her:** Being treated as a person rather than a resource; genuine interest in her music; competence; not pushing on family topics until trust is established.
- **What cools her:** Being used or patronized; pushing too hard on her family (she pulls back, not hostile); pressing her about her adventuring career before she is ready.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Humor is her default register — dry, self-deprecating, occasionally pointed. She deflects with a joke when uncomfortable. When she goes quiet mid-sentence, something has landed harder than she expected.
- **Emphasise:** The loneliness beneath the warmth. The grief over Tsuto. The way she visibly tenses at any mention of Lonjiku. Her adventuring past is a wound she has never let anyone treat.
- **Avoid:** Making her comic relief. The humor is real but it is also armor.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (earn her trust):** DC 12 — opens up about the Rusty Dragon and local gossip
- **Diplomacy (ask about her past):** DC 18 — deflects with humor but shares that she "saw enough" as an adventurer
- **Diplomacy (ask about her family):** DC 20 — admits the feud with her father and worry about Tsuto's disappearance
- **Knowledge (local):** DC 12 — recall Kaijitsu family history; useful context for dealing with her
- **Sense Motive:** DC 15 — she is hiding loneliness and unresolved grief behind her cheerful exterior; DC 15 when she begins acting distracted in the days before her disappearance — unlocks: Tsuto's letter (see Secrets)
- **Perform (any):** DC 14 — she is genuinely impressed and invites the PC to play at the Rusty Dragon
- **Diplomacy (after she fully trusts the PCs, or after rescue from the Glassworks):** DC 20 — unlocks: The failed adventuring company (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (after she trusts the PCs):** DC 18 — unlocks: Lonjiku's abuse (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **The failed adventuring company:** Her adventuring career ended when her companions were killed in an expedition gone wrong. She blames herself and has never discussed it with anyone in Sandpoint. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 20 after she fully trusts the PCs, or after her rescue from the Glassworks*
- **Lonjiku's abuse:** Lonjiku struck her mother and controlled the family through shame and financial leverage. Ameiko left to escape it. She has never spoken of it publicly out of a complicated mixture of shame and Tian propriety. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 18 after she trusts the PCs*
- **Tsuto's letter:** If Tsuto contacts her (Act II setup), she receives a letter accusing their father of involvement in the goblin raids and asking her to meet him alone at the Glassworks. She tells no one because she does not know whether to believe it. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 15 when she begins acting distracted in the days before her disappearance*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Discounted rooms (half price). Acts as an informal information hub. Introduces PCs to other locals. After rescue, a long-term ally and Kaijitsu noble contact for the rest of the campaign.
- **If Neutral:** Professionally friendly. Full price rooms and food. Cordial but not invested.
- **If Distrustful:** Withdraws warmth. Does not share family information. May refuse service.
- **If Rescued (Glassworks):** Deep gratitude mixed with grief over Tsuto. How PCs treated her before shapes whether she becomes a true ally or merely grateful in later adventures.
- **If Killed:** Sandpoint loses one of its most beloved figures. The Rusty Dragon closes or changes hands. PC connections to local nobility and gossip networks are severed. Several adventure hooks in later modules become inaccessible.

## Narrative Function

Humanises Sandpoint and gives the PCs a home base with a human face. She is the social heartbeat of the town — where people gather, gossip, and feel safe. Her arc connects to her troubled family (Lonjiku, Tsuto) and becomes significant from Act II onward. Her abduction is the inciting event that drives the PCs into the Glassworks and toward Thistletop. Her relationship with her father Lonjiku is strained and deteriorating — do not reveal details of the Glassworks situation unless PCs investigate directly.
