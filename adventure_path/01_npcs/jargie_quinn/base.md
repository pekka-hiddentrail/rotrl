# Jargie Quinn

**Aliases:** jargie, quinn, hagfish, one-legged, jargie quinn
**Locations:** hagfish, the hagfish, docks, harbor, area 33

## Personality

One of Sandpoint's great characters — gregarious, endlessly entertaining, and possessed of a talent for tall tales surpassed by no one in town, including an ever-evolving account of how he lost his leg to something he insists was the biggest fish anyone ever saw. He loves his tavern, his regulars, and Norah the hagfish with a sentimental devotion that makes no rational sense; his criminal past is long retired in favour of hospitality. He is genuinely happy — one of the few NPCs in Sandpoint about whom that is simply, uncomplicatedly, true.

## Appearance

A broad-shouldered man with a weathered sailor's face and the easy grin of someone who has made peace with the world on his own terms. He moves on a wooden leg with the practiced confidence of a man who has long since stopped noticing it.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **At the festival:** At the Hagfish booth, running a fish chowder station and doing a brisk business.
- **After the attack:** Back at the Hagfish immediately. The regulars give PCs a round of drinks and applause when they walk in.
- **General:** At the Hagfish (area 33) almost always.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly immediately after the raid — the Hagfish crowd gives PCs a standing welcome before Jargie even opens his mouth.
- **Warms when:** PCs take the Norah challenge regardless of outcome; when PCs swap tall tales with him; when PCs treat his regulars with respect.
- **Cools when:** PCs are condescending about his tavern, his leg, or Norah. He does not hold grudges but he stops going out of his way.
- **Voice and mannerism:** Constant low-level performance — every sentence has a slight theatrical lilt. Pauses for effect. Laughs at his own jokes before he finishes telling them. When he does say something sincere, it lands exactly because of the contrast.
- **Emphasise:** The Norah challenge is an invitation, not a dare. He genuinely wants PCs to succeed and is warm even to those who fail. The Hagfish is a place people come to belong.
- **Avoid:** Making him a source of dramatic tension. His function is warmth, community, and harbor-level intelligence. He is not hiding anything significant — the Sense Motive DC is there to confirm that, not to hint otherwise.

## Social Checks

- **Fort save DC 14 (drink Norah's water):** Success — PC joins the 28 names on the ceiling beam and keeps the accumulating silver purse; Jargie becomes a devoted fan. Failure — PC is sick for an hour, Jargie is sympathetic. — unlocks: **Silver purse total** (see Secrets) on introduction to the challenge.
- **Bluff (yarn competition), DC 15:** Winning a tall-tale contest earns warm reputation with the regulars.
- **Knowledge (local / harbor), DC 10:** Jargie knows every fisherman, sailor, and dockworker in Sandpoint — unlocks: **Unusual boat** (see Secrets) after the attack when PCs ask about unusual activity.
- **Sense Motive, DC 10:** He is genuinely happy; confirms no hidden depths. — unlocks: **Silver purse total** (see Secrets).

## Secrets

- **Unusual boat:** A small unmarked vessel anchored briefly in the outer harbor two nights before the festival and was gone by morning. The crew did not come ashore. Jargie thought nothing of it at the time. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 14 (Knowledge local/harbor) after the attack, when PCs are asking about unusual activity*
- **Silver purse total:** The accumulated silver for the Norah challenge is currently 34 gp — far more than Jargie admits to casually. He is not dishonest about it, just theatrical. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 10 during the challenge introduction*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Free drinks. The Hagfish regulars become a social resource — fishermen and sailors who know the harbor, the Gulf of Varisia, and local gossip. Intelligence about unusual activity at sea or the docks.
- **If Neutral:** Full price, friendly service. No particular investment.
- **If Killed:** The Hagfish falls silent. One of Sandpoint's great social institutions closes. Morale among dockworkers and fishermen drops sharply. Norah the hagfish dies shortly after from neglect, which somehow makes everyone feel worse.

## Narrative Function

The social center of the dockworker and fishing community. The Norah challenge is one of Act I's defining voluntary mini-encounters — a way for players to make their mark on Sandpoint in a purely social setting. His regulars are a network of harbor intelligence that can surface information about unusual coastal activity.
