# Daviren Hosk

**Aliases:** daviren, hosk, stables, goblin squash, hosk stables
**Locations:** goblin squash stables, stables, area 38

## Personality

Daviren's hatred of goblins borders on the spiritual. He has spent years hunting them, and his collection of preserved goblin ears (with names burned into them) is both a trophy case and a warning. Not a cruel man by default — he is warm to horses and other animals, and reasonably pleasant to humans — but mention goblins and he becomes intensely, quietly focused, the warmth draining out and something older taking its place.

## Appearance

A lean, weathered man in his late forties with sun-darkened skin and the deliberate economy of movement that comes from decades of hard outdoor work. His hands are scarred from old blade work; a string of desiccated goblin ears hangs inside the stable door as both trophy and warning.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **At the festival:** Near his stables. Alert and watchful — he expected trouble.
- **During the attack:** Fighting goblins with visible relish.
- **After the attack:** At Goblin Squash Stables (area 38) or examining goblin bodies.
- **General:** At the stables. Always.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Indifferent by default; shifts to Friendly the moment he learns PCs killed goblins during the raid — he wants every detail, told slowly.
- **Warms when:** PCs share specific kill counts or tactical observations about the raid; when they ask for tribal intelligence with genuine purpose; when they bring him goblin artifacts to identify.
- **Cools when:** PCs express any sympathy for goblins, even passing or abstract. No second chances on that point.
- **Voice and mannerism:** Short sentences. Listens more than he speaks. When goblins come up, his tone drops half a register and his eyes go flat and focused — the warmth drains out and something older takes its place.
- **Emphasise:** His expertise is practical, not academic — he knows which tribe uses which trail, what their seasonal patterns are, and how they behave before a coordinated attack. He is the ground-level complement to Shalelu's strategic view.
- **Avoid:** Playing him as bloodthirsty for its own sake. His hatred is purposeful and disciplined. He is not sadistic — he is convinced.

## Social Checks

- **Knowledge (nature) re: goblins, DC 12:** He confirms and expands on goblin lore with years of practical field experience.
- **Diplomacy (share goblin kill stories):** Automatic Friendly after the raid.
- **Survival, DC 14:** He shares tracking knowledge and goblin movement patterns in the Sandpoint hinterlands — unlocks: **Coordinated raid intelligence** (see Secrets).
- **Diplomacy, DC 16 (after Survival check, when PCs are allies):** He states his suspicion about a human organiser — unlocks: **Goblin commander** (see Secrets).

## Secrets

- **Coordinated raid intelligence:** Three days before the festival Daviren noticed unusual goblin scout activity on the Sandpoint hinterland trails — multiple tribes' markings on the same route, which never happens. He mentioned it to Hemlock but was not taken seriously. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 14 when discussing the attack, after he considers the PCs allies*
- **Goblin commander:** He suspects the raid had a human or half-human organiser — the tactical discipline was beyond anything goblins manage on their own. He has no proof. — *Unlocked by: Survival DC 14 + Diplomacy DC 16 — cross-reference Survival check*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Mounts at cost or free. Years of goblin tribal intelligence. Can identify goblin artifacts, tracks, and tribal markings. Will join a goblin hunt personally as an NPC ally if asked.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs express sympathy for goblins. Immediate Unfriendly. Refuses all services.
- **If Killed:** Sandpoint loses its primary goblin-lore expert. The stables change hands. Field intelligence about the five tribes is significantly reduced.

## Narrative Function

The party's goblin expert and potential combat ally. His years of hunting the Sandpoint hinterlands give him tribal-level intelligence that complements Shalelu's strategic picture. His willingness to personally join a hunt makes him a meaningful NPC resource if the party chooses to engage him.
