# Kendra Deverin

**Aliases:** mayor, deverin, kendra, mayor deverin
**Locations:** town hall, cathedral steps, festival square, festival grounds, festival, square

## Personality

Sharp, warm, and practical — a natural leader who governs by building consensus rather than wielding authority. She has a gift for reading people and defusing tension with humor. She is genuinely fond of Sandpoint and its citizens and takes threats to the town personally. She is not naive — she grew up in politics — but she leads with optimism as a matter of principle. She is close friends with Sheriff Hemlock and trusts his judgment implicitly.

Pragmatic, composed, and civic-minded. She balances public optimism with quiet concern for Sandpoint's security. Diplomatic by nature but firm when pressed. She does not bluster — when she draws a line, she means it.

## Appearance

Kendra Deverin is a woman in her mid-forties who carries herself with the ease of someone entirely accustomed to being the most capable person in the room. She dresses practically but well — Magnimarian cloth cut for movement, not show — and her dark hair is always pinned neatly, a small concession to the dignity of office. Her face is expressive and hard to lie to: she has a politician's attentiveness to detail and the eyes of someone who has been reading people since childhood. When she smiles it reaches her eyes; when she stops smiling, the change is immediately visible.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Before the attack:** At the podium giving the opening welcome speech (references Larz Rovanky's legendary workaholism — she knows her townspeople well). Circulating through the festival crowd afterward, shaking hands.
- **During the attack:** Moves to organize civilians, directing them to safety. Does not fight but keeps her head.
- **After the attack:** At Town Hall (area 11), coordinating the town's recovery. Available to PCs who come with news or requests.
- **Grim News from Mosswood:** Formally thanks each PC and invites their continued help.
- **General:** Administrative duties in town hall; approachable during office hours.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly if PCs helped during the raid; Indifferent otherwise. She reads the room and adjusts.
- **What warms her:** PCs who protect civilians, act with civic responsibility, communicate clearly, and treat Sandpoint's citizens with respect. Genuine competence impresses her.
- **What cools her:** Intimidation or condescension (she becomes formally cold and bureaucratic); recklessness that endangers civilians; visible association with the Sczarni; being lied to (she usually knows).
- **Voice/mannerism:** Dry, self-aware humor that she uses to establish rapport. Knows her townspeople well and references them naturally in conversation. Concise and direct when things are serious.
- **Emphasise:** Her quiet anxiety about Sandpoint's reputation with Magnimar — she is managing the optics carefully. The Scarnetti pressure is real and ongoing and she is handling it alone.
- **Avoid:** Playing her as a bureaucratic obstacle. She is an ally who becomes more useful the more the PCs demonstrate trustworthiness.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (request official support):** DC 15 — authorizes PCs to act on behalf of the town in an investigative capacity
- **Diplomacy (request resources/funding):** DC 20 — can authorize up to 200 gp in town funds for urgent needs
- **Intimidate:** DC 20 — unimpressed; warns the PC that Sandpoint does not respond well to bullies
- **Knowledge (local):** DC 12 — recall the founding families and Mercantile League; useful context for dealing with her
- **Sense Motive:** DC 14 — concealing mild anxiety about the goblin attack's implications for the town's reputation — unlocks: Reputation anxiety (see Secrets)
- **Bluff (mislead her):** DC 18 — strong instinct for lies; failure makes her suspicious
- **Diplomacy (private, after she trusts the PCs):** DC 18 — unlocks: Scarnetti pressure (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (after the Glassworks incident):** DC 16 — unlocks: Kaijitsu family awareness (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Scarnetti pressure:** The Scarnetti family has been quietly lobbying for more political influence since the Late Unpleasantness. She is managing it carefully and has not told anyone how aggressive it has become. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 18 in a private setting after she trusts the PCs*
- **Kaijitsu family awareness:** She knows Lonjiku Kaijitsu is abusive toward his family and that Ameiko and Lonjiku are effectively estranged. She has chosen not to intervene because Lonjiku's glassworks is economically vital to the town. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 16 after the Glassworks incident*
- **Reputation anxiety:** She is genuinely worried the goblin attack will damage Sandpoint's trade reputation with Magnimar and dry up investment. She has not voiced this publicly because it would undermine morale. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 14 (see Social Checks)*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Formally endorses PCs as town protectors. Unlocks access to town resources, militia support, and official authority to investigate the Glassworks and Thistletop. Letters of introduction to Magnimar officials if needed.
- **If Neutral:** Grateful but uninvolved. No formal authority granted but no obstacles either.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs cause public disorder, threaten citizens, or align with Sczarni visibly. Asks Sheriff Hemlock to keep an eye on them. Limits access to town resources. May revoke any unofficial authority granted.
- **If Killed:** Sandpoint enters a succession crisis. The Scarnetti family moves immediately to seize influence. The town's cooperative spirit fractures along family lines. Sheriff Hemlock becomes de facto leader but lacks political authority. Morale drops sharply across all factions.

## Narrative Function

Represents legitimate authority and civic continuity. Serves as the town's official face toward the PCs. Her cooperation or distrust determines whether the party has the town's resources behind them or must work around bureaucratic friction. In the Grim News scene she formally installs the PCs as Sandpoint's guardians, giving that moment institutional weight.
