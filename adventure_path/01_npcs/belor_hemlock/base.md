# Belor Hemlock

**Aliases:** sheriff, hemlock, belor, sheriff hemlock
**Locations:** sheriff's office, garrison, festival square, festival grounds, square, town hall

## Personality

Hemlock is a man who carries weight. He inherited the sheriff's position under the worst possible circumstances — the night Chopper was finally stopped — and has been proving himself worthy of it ever since. He is dour in public, economical with words, and visibly uncomfortable with celebration, but beneath that he is deeply honorable and fiercely protective of the town.

Disciplined, professional, and quietly burdened. He respects competence and despises grandstanding. Not cold — he simply has no patience for politics or posturing. If the PCs prove themselves in a crisis, he will treat them as partners rather than civilians.

He is Shoanti by birth, having changed his name to its Chelish equivalent in a gesture of integration that cost him his relationship with his brother. He regrets it but will not undo it.

## Appearance

Hemlock has a Shoanti's broad frame and weathered complexion, carrying his size with the deliberate economy of someone who has learned not to waste effort. His face is all planes and controlled expression — not unfriendly, but never off-duty. He wears the Sandpoint sheriff's badge as though it were something he earned rather than inherited, because that is exactly how he thinks of it. When he is still, he is very still; when he moves, he moves with purpose.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Before the attack:** At the podium giving his speech (the one that brings the crowd down a notch). Afterward circulates the festival perimeter, watching for trouble.
- **During the attack:** Immediately in command. Directing guards, organizing the defense.
- **After the attack:** At the Garrison (area 10) or the festival square assessing damage. Approaches PCs to thank them.
- **Grim News from Mosswood:** Seeks PCs out personally to join the meeting. Departs for Magnimar shortly after.
- **Absent (after Grim News):** Leaves town with only a skeleton garrison. Returns days later with only a dozen green soldiers. His implicit intention in asking PCs to maintain a presence — they are now Sandpoint's primary protectors.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Indifferent/Professional. He is not unfriendly, but he does not extend trust by default.
- **What warms him:** PCs acting decisively without causing collateral damage; competence in a crisis; treating civilians as the priority; showing knowledge of or respect for Shoanti culture.
- **What cools him:** Grandstanding; lying to him (he will notice); visible association with the Sczarni; causing property damage or civilian harm; attempting to intimidate him (automatic hostility).
- **Voice/mannerism:** Short declarative sentences. No filler. Sustained eye contact. Silences mean he is thinking, not finished.
- **Emphasise:** His wariness about the goblin coordination — it is bothering him more than he is letting on. His respect for the PCs if they earn it is real and meaningful; do not undersell it.
- **Avoid:** Making him seem merely cold. He is contained, not empty. The warmth is there; it is simply rationed.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (earn his respect):** DC 15 — opens up about goblin threat intelligence and the five tribes
- **Diplomacy (ask about Chopper):** DC 18 — will talk about it, briefly and without details, if asked respectfully
- **Intimidate:** Automatic fail — completely unintimidated; attempting it makes him hostile
- **Knowledge (local) re: his background:** DC 12 — recall his Shoanti background and name change; mentioning it respectfully earns a point of trust
- **Sense Motive:** DC 16 — he is more worried about the goblin situation than he lets on publicly; DC 18 when Tesarani is mentioned — unlocks: Romantic attachment (see Secrets)
- **Bluff:** DC 20 — a lawman's instinct for evasion; very hard to mislead
- **Diplomacy (private, after trust established):** DC 20 — unlocks: Romantic attachment (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (after he treats PCs as partners):** DC 16 — unlocks: Goblin intelligence withheld (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (after PCs have earned his trust):** DC 18 — unlocks: Shoanti estrangement (see Secrets)
- **Knowledge (local) DC 16 + direct respectful question:** — unlocks: Shoanti estrangement (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Romantic attachment:** He is quietly involved with Kaye Tesarani (the brothel owner, area 43). He considers it entirely private and would be mortified if it became town gossip. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 18 when Tesarani is mentioned, or Diplomacy DC 20 in a private context*
- **Shoanti estrangement:** His name change from his Shoanti birth name to its Chelish equivalent permanently damaged his relationship with his brother. He regrets it but will not undo it. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 18 after he trusts the PCs, or Knowledge (local) DC 16 + direct respectful question*
- **Goblin intelligence withheld:** He knows more than he's said publicly — his scouts have reported unusual coordination between tribes that suggests a single organiser. He hasn't shared this because he doesn't want to cause panic before he has proof. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 16 after he treats PCs as partners*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Deputizes PCs (informal authority to investigate and act). Shares intelligence on goblin tribes and the Sczarni. Fast-tracks any legal complications PCs encounter. Provides a guard escort if needed.
- **If Neutral:** Professional courtesy. Will not obstruct but will not actively support.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs lie to him, break laws visibly, or associate with Sczarni. Puts them under informal watch. Any legal trouble gets no benefit of the doubt. May bar them from the Garrison.
- **If Absent (Magnimar):** Sandpoint left with thin coverage. PCs are the primary protectors — his implicit intention.
- **If Killed:** Law and order collapses rapidly. The Sczarni (Jubrayl Vhiski) move to fill the vacuum. The militia is leaderless. Mayor Deverin is forced into an impossible position. Garridan Viskalai at the White Deer is privately devastated.

## Narrative Function

Embodies law and order under strain. Acts as a professional counterpoint to the PCs — methodical where they may be impulsive, measured where they may be reckless. His cooperation is a meaningful resource: access to the garrison, intelligence, and institutional legitimacy. His departure for Magnimar is the moment the PCs formally inherit responsibility for Sandpoint's safety.
