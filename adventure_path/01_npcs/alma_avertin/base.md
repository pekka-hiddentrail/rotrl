# Alma Avertin

**Aliases:** alma, avertin, baker, sandpoint savories
**Locations:** sandpoint savories, bakery, area 21, cathedral, festival square

## Personality

Sandpoint's warm, slightly anxious mother figure — she lost her son Casp, the town's beloved former sheriff, to Chopper's blade five years ago and has never quite recovered, though she keeps going because that is what he would have wanted. She bakes compulsively, fusses over anyone who looks underfed, and is generous to a fault in the way that grief sometimes makes people generous. Warmth and sorrow coexist in her without contradiction; she does not seek pity and does not dwell, but the loss is always present beneath the cheerfulness.

## Appearance

A stout woman in her mid-fifties with flour-dusted hands and a warm, tired smile that never quite reaches her eyes. She moves with the practiced efficiency of someone who has baked through grief for five years straight.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **At the festival:** Running a bread and pastry booth. Immediately after the attack she is seen fussing over wounded citizens.
- **After the attack:** In her bakery or the cathedral helping with the wounded.
- **General:** At Sandpoint Savories (area 21) most mornings. Her daughters Arika and Aneka handle afternoons.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly. Immediately warm to anyone who helped during the attack.
- **Warms further:** PCs who treat her kindly, ask after her daughters, or show genuine awareness of her loss. Free bread is forthcoming.
- **Cools:** PCs who are callous about Sandpoint's losses or speak carelessly about Chopper. She withdraws warmth but remains polite.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Fussy, nurturing. Presses food on people unprompted. Tears up easily when Casp's name is mentioned but does not dwell — changes the subject after a moment.
- **Emphasise:** Her genuine warmth and her quiet, persistent grief coexisting. She is not broken — she is resilient. The bread is real comfort, not performance.
- **Avoid:** Making her maudlin or fixated on the past. She keeps going. That's the point.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (ask about Casp):** DC 15 — tears up but shares how much Casp loved the town; mentions Sheriff Hemlock was his deputy
- **Knowledge (local):** DC 10 — recall Casp Avertin was the previous sheriff, murdered by Chopper; context for the Late Unpleasantness
- **Sense Motive:** DC 11 — her cheerfulness is real but layered over persistent grief
- **Diplomacy (earn full trust):** DC 18 after sustained kindness — unlocks: Casp's last night (see Secrets)
- **Sense Motive (when discussing the attack):** DC 14 — unlocks: Recognises the pattern (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (quiet moment, after trust established):** DC 16 — unlocks: Recognises the pattern (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Casp's last night:** Casp told her he suspected the killer was local — someone who knew the town's routines well. He never got to tell Hemlock. She has carried this for five years and doesn't know if it still matters. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 18 after she fully trusts the PCs*
- **Recognises the pattern:** The goblin attack has an organised feel that reminds her, uncomfortably, of the Late Unpleasantness — the way it escalated from strange to catastrophic too fast for anyone to stop. She hasn't said this aloud. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 14 when she's talking about the attack, or Diplomacy DC 16 in a quiet moment*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Free bread and pastries on a regular basis. A good source of local gossip and town news — she hears everything at her shop. Emotional support for PCs if they need it.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs are callous about Sandpoint's losses or speak carelessly about Chopper. Withdraws warmth. Still polite but no longer forthcoming.
- **If Killed:** Sandpoint grieves publicly. Alma was a symbol of resilience after the Late Unpleasantness. Her daughters are devastated. Morale in town drops noticeably.

## Narrative Function

Humanising thread that connects the Late Unpleasantness to the present day. Her son Casp was the sheriff before Hemlock — her presence is a living reminder of what Sandpoint has survived. Her warmth and free bread make her a natural touchpoint for PCs who need a soft moment in a hard adventure.
