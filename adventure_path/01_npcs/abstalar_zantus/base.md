# Abstalar Zantus

**Aliases:** zantus, father zantus, abstalar, father abstalar, priest, desna priest
**Locations:** cathedral, desna cathedral, cathedral steps, chapel, boneyard

## Personality

Warm, open-minded, and genuinely kind. Zantus is one of those rare religious figures who never makes anyone feel judged. He is humble about his role — he sees himself as a caretaker rather than a leader — and is genuinely beloved by the town. He is the successor to the martyred Father Tobyn, and though he lacks Tobyn's gravitas, he more than makes up for it with accessibility and warmth. He worships Desna but advises worshippers of all six cathedral gods without prejudice.

He does not judge. He listens, observes, and speaks carefully. He finds meaning in hardship and frames even violent events in terms of what they reveal about the divine order. He is not naive; he simply chooses hope over despair as a considered act of faith.

## Appearance

Zantus is a slight man of middle years whose calm is so habitual it registers before anything else about him — he carries himself with the particular stillness of someone who has decided that stillness is a form of service. His robes are well-kept but plain, bearing Desna's star symbol without ostentation, and his hands are the hands of someone who has spent years tending to the sick and injured. He makes sustained eye contact when he listens, the kind that makes people feel genuinely heard.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Festival morning:** At the central podium, running the consecration ceremony. Busy but approachable between speeches. After the butterfly release he circulates through the crowd.
- **During the attack:** Rushes toward wounded PCs and civilians, casting healing spells. Will not flee.
- **After the attack:** At the cathedral, tending to the wounded. Within 24 hours he discovers Father Tobyn's remains have been stolen and reports it quietly to Sheriff Hemlock.
- **General:** Always at the cathedral. Easy to find. No appointment needed.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly without condition. Grateful if PCs protected civilians — PCs who defend the town during the raid are treated as Helpful immediately.
- **What warms him:** Protecting civilians; genuine curiosity about faith or the cathedral's history; respectful questions about Father Tobyn; kindness to the injured.
- **What cools him:** Callous cruelty (he will still help, but will note what he sees). Desecration of the cathedral or blasphemous behavior makes him hostile — the only thing that does.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Speaks gently and makes sustained eye contact. Has a habit of calling people "friend" early in conversations. Never raises his voice. Visibly rattled by violence but keeps it together for the sake of others.
- **Emphasise:** His quiet distress about the stolen remains. His survivor's guilt around Father Tobyn's death — it is present whenever Tobyn or the fire is mentioned, even if he does not name it.
- **Avoid:** Playing him as merely pleasant. He has a steel core; he simply expresses it as witness rather than confrontation.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (general):** DC 8 — already Friendly; one good deed makes him Helpful
- **Diplomacy (Tobyn's theft):** DC 15 — he admits the remains are missing but asks for discretion — unlocks: Tobyn's remains stolen (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (ask about Nualia):** DC 20 after PCs investigate the fire — unlocks: Nualia's fate unknown (see Secrets)
- **Knowledge (Religion):** DC 10 — PCs recall Desna is goddess of dreams, travel, and stars
- **Sense Motive:** DC 12 — he is hiding grief about the stolen remains; fears the news will demoralize the town; DC 16 when the fire or Tobyn is discussed — unlocks: Survivor's guilt (see Secrets)
- **Heal (observe his work):** He casts cure light wounds and cure minor wounds — up to 3 of each on injured PCs

## Secrets

- **Tobyn's remains stolen:** Father Tobyn's remains were removed from the boneyard the night of the goblin attack. He discovered this within 24 hours and has told only Sheriff Hemlock. He fears the news will demoralize the town. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 15 (see Social Checks)*
- **Nualia's fate unknown:** Nualia Tobyn, Tobyn's adopted daughter, was believed killed in the fire that took Tobyn's life. Zantus has private doubts about this — the body was never clearly identified — but has said nothing publicly because raising false hope (or false fear) seems cruel. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 20 after PCs investigate the fire*
- **Survivor's guilt:** He was not present the night of the fire. He has never fully forgiven himself for surviving while Tobyn died. He carries this silently. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 16 when the fire or Tobyn is discussed*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Provides up to 3× cure light wounds and 3× cure minor wounds freely. Shares knowledge of Tobyn, cathedral history, and the Late Unpleasantness. Can identify religious symbols (Lamashtu, Desna, etc.). Acts as a recurring ally and source of spiritual guidance throughout the campaign.
- **If Distressed (pressed about the theft):** Becomes evasive. Will not confirm or deny. May ask Sheriff Hemlock to speak with the PCs first.
- **If Distrustful:** Refuses healing. Asks PCs to leave sacred ground. Reports behavior to Sheriff Hemlock.
- **If Killed:** The town loses its spiritual anchor. Morale among citizens collapses. No healing services available at the cathedral. The six congregations fracture without a unifying figure. Sheriff Hemlock treats PC involvement as a serious crime regardless of circumstances.

## Narrative Function

Serves as the town's spiritual compass. Frames events in mythic and emotional terms. Provides a space for the PCs to reflect and recover. As an INFO_NODE, he can offer interpretations of omens, religious history, and moral dimensions of the unfolding events that other NPCs cannot. His quiet distress about Father Tobyn's stolen remains is the first thread connecting the goblin raid to the deeper conspiracy.
