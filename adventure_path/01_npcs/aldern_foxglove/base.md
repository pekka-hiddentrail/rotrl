# Aldern Foxglove

**Aliases:** aldern, foxglove, aldern foxglove, noble, nobleman
**Locations:** rusty dragon, inn, festival grounds, festival square, white deer

## Personality

On the surface, Aldern is charming, generous, and eager to please — a wealthy nobleman from Magnimar who seems genuinely impressed by the PCs and grateful to be alive. He is well-traveled, well-read, and a fascinating conversationalist. He is also deeply, dangerously unstable, and his admiration for one PC in particular tips quickly from gratitude into something unsettling.

He attaches quickly and intensely to people who impress him. His gratitude can feel overwhelming if you pay attention. The more the GM plays him as harmless and likeable now, the more devastating his reveal in "The Skinsaw Murders" will be.

## Appearance

Well-dressed in the Magnimaran aristocrat style, with a warm smile and easy social grace that makes him effortlessly pleasant company. He asks personal questions with an intensity that is almost flattering — at first.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **During the attack:** Cowering behind a rain barrel in front of the White Deer as a goblin commando kills his dog. Rescued by the PCs in the "Die Dog Die" encounter.
- **After the attack:** Staying at the Rusty Dragon. Seeks out the PCs within 1d3 days if they don't find him first.
- **The Boar Hunt:** Invites PCs to Tickwood for a boar hunt, buying them horses. His obsession with one PC should grow noticeably over the course of the hunt.
- **General:** Sandpoint visitor — not a permanent resident. Will leave for Magnimar after a few days.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly, openly grateful. Focuses particular attention on the most striking or impressive PC.
- **Warms further:** Any PC who accepts his generosity warmly or spends genuine time with him. His attachment intensifies with positive attention.
- **Cools:** Being rebuffed or dismissed. He masks disappointment well and withdraws — but the obsession grows in absence.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Warm, curious, slightly too intense with his questions. Speaks with Magnimaran polish. Laughs easily. Deflects any question about Foxglove Manor or his finances with a practiced joke.
- **Emphasise:** His genuine charm and likability. He should feel like a friend. Play him as pleasant background — never threatening, never significant.
- **Avoid:** Any hint of menace or instability. His danger only becomes apparent in hindsight. The players should look back and see it — not forward.
- **Critical GM note:** Never make him seem threatening or significant. His danger only becomes apparent in hindsight. Play him as pleasant background.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (casual conversation):** He is delightful company and shares Magnimar high society stories freely
- **Knowledge (local):** DC 15 — PCs may recall the Foxglove family has a manor south of town on the Lost Coast, known locally as "Foxglove Manor" or "the Misgivings" — unlocks: Foxglove Manor's history (see Secrets)
- **Sense Motive (during boar hunt):** DC 16 — something about his attention feels wrong; admiration edged with need, not quite natural
- **Sense Motive (after boar hunt):** DC 20 — PCs realize his interest has passed from gratitude into obsession — unlocks: Growing obsession (see Secrets)
- **Bluff (redirect his attention):** DC 18 — temporarily successful, but he always returns his focus
- **Knowledge (nobility):** DC 18 — unlocks: Financial ruin (see Secrets)
- **Knowledge (local, Magnimar):** DC 16 — unlocks: Financial ruin (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Financial ruin:** Aldern is deeply in debt to the Brothers of the Seven in Magnimar, a front for the Skinsaw cult. His generosity with the PCs is partly genuine and partly a desperate attempt to feel like his old self before everything collapses. — *Unlocked by: Knowledge (nobility) DC 18 or Knowledge (local, Magnimar) DC 16*
- **Foxglove Manor's history:** The Misgivings has a dark history — previous generations of Foxgloves died under disturbing circumstances. Aldern knows about it and avoids thinking about it. He will deflect any direct question with a joke. — *Unlocked by: Knowledge (local) DC 15 — cross-reference existing check*
- **Growing obsession:** He is already fixating on one PC. By the end of his time in Sandpoint the fixation has a compulsive quality he cannot fully control or explain. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 20 during the boar hunt — cross-reference existing check*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative (befriended):** Provides 50 gp reward. Horses for the boar hunt. Access to Magnimar social connections. Plants seeds of the next adventure. Obsession forms — becomes the foundation of "The Skinsaw Murders."
- **If Fixated (boar hunt):** His attention on one PC becomes the engine of Book II. The more real the connection feels now, the better the payoff later.
- **If Avoided:** He still departs for Magnimar, but his obsession grows in absence rather than presence. The next adventure's revelation hits differently — they barely knew him.
- **If Killed in Act I:** "The Skinsaw Murders" loses its primary villain. The undead Foxglove cannot be created. Several subsequent adventure hooks collapse.

## Narrative Function

Seeds the horror of Book II through mundane, pleasant interaction. His importance in Act I is entirely social. The GM must never make him seem threatening or significant — his danger only becomes apparent in hindsight. He is charming because the story needs the players to like him before it uses him against them.
