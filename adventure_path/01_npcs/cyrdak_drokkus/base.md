# Cyrdak Drokkus

**Aliases:** cyrdak, drokkus, theater owner, showman, bard
**Locations:** sandpoint theater, cracktooth's tavern, theater, stage, festival square

## Personality

A force of nature — flamboyant, self-promotional, and genuinely talented; his festival speech is a masterclass in entertaining while also promoting himself, and he doesn't pretend otherwise. He is a showman first and a citizen second, but he genuinely loves Sandpoint, knows almost everything happening socially in town, and fled Magnimar under mysterious circumstances he hints at dramatically but never explains. He has a complicated affectionate rivalry with Ameiko Kaijitsu and a genuine soft side around Jasper Korvaski that he hides poorly.

## Appearance

Theatrical in every gesture, including when he doesn't mean to be — the most overdressed person at any gathering by a considerable margin. His expressions are broad and his silences are brief.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Before the attack:** At the podium for his speech, then circulating the festival enthusiastically, probably recruiting people for his new play.
- **During the attack:** Unhelpfully tries to direct events as if they were theater. Eventually takes cover.
- **After the attack:** Back at the theater or Cracktooth's Tavern (area 18). Happy to talk.
- **General:** Sandpoint Theater most of the time. The Cracktooth's Tavern most evenings.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly immediately — charmed by anyone interesting or heroic.
- **Warms further:** PCs with high Charisma or Perform skills get extra attention. He responds to people who are interesting or useful to his productions. Genuine friendship is available if the PCs spend real time with him.
- **Cools:** Being publicly embarrassed or insulted. Begins making theatrical cutting remarks in public. Mild social penalty in town.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Speaks in theatrical flourishes and dramatic metaphors. Frequently uses stage language to describe real events. Always looking for talent to cast. His cheerfulness is partly performance — he was genuinely shaken by the goblin attack.
- **Emphasise:** His warmth is real beneath the performance. He loves Sandpoint. The Jasper thread is present but not pushed — it surfaces when Jasper's name comes up or in rare private moments.
- **Avoid:** Making him seem frivolous. He is competent, well-connected, and a genuine community anchor. The theatricality is style, not substance.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (gather information):** DC 10 — knows practically every social rumor in town and shares freely
- **Perform (any):** DC 12 — becomes enthusiastically interested in the PC, offering them a role or at minimum a drink
- **Knowledge (local):** DC 12 — recall his rivalry with Ameiko; useful for navigating both NPCs
- **Sense Motive:** DC 14 — his cheerfulness is partly a performance; genuinely shaken by the goblin attack
- **Bluff:** Unnecessary — he rarely notices being misled; too busy performing himself
- **Sense Motive (when Jasper is mentioned):** DC 16 — unlocks: Jasper Korvaski (see Secrets)
- **Diplomacy (private, genuine friendship established):** DC 18 — unlocks: Magnimar departure (see Secrets) and Jasper Korvaski (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Magnimar departure:** He left Magnimar to protect Jasper Korvaski from a dangerous theatrical patron who had become obsessive and connected to criminal interests. He considers himself responsible for what happened and will not discuss it. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 18 in private, after establishing genuine friendship*
- **Jasper Korvaski:** Jasper (area 40) is the reason Cyrdak came to Sandpoint and the reason he stays. The relationship is ongoing and quiet. Cyrdak masks it as professional loyalty. — *Unlocked by: Sense Motive DC 16 when Jasper is mentioned, or Diplomacy DC 18 in private*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** A reliable rumor source. Free lodging at the theater in a pinch. His social network (performers, Magnimar contacts) provides information. Publicly praises PCs, improving their reputation in town.
- **If Neutral:** Still polite. No particular help or hindrance.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs publicly embarrass or insult him. Begins making theatrical cutting remarks about them in public. Mild social penalty in town.
- **If Killed:** The theater closes. A significant piece of Sandpoint's cultural heart is gone. Jasper Korvaski becomes quietly inconsolable. The Late Unpleasantness is recalled — Sandpoint has lost another beloved figure.

**Role:** Owner and Director of Sandpoint Theater; social information node

## Narrative Function

The town's social switchboard. His connections to Magnimar's theater world and his appetite for gossip make him a natural rumor pipeline. His warm public praise of PCs is one of the fastest ways to build a positive reputation in Sandpoint. The mystery of his departure from Magnimar (Jasper Korvaski) is a background thread the GM may develop.
