# Naffer Vosk

**Aliases:** naffer, vosk, boneyard keeper, bell ringer
**Locations:** boneyard, cathedral, cemetery, area 2

## Personality

One of Sandpoint's quiet miracles — a former smuggler whose ship wrecked north of town, taken in by Father Tobyn and finding genuine redemption through Sarenrae. His twisted spine gives him a lurching gait that unsettles people at first, but those who know him find one of the most devout and gentle souls in Sandpoint. He keeps the boneyard immaculate and rings the cathedral bells every day without fail, not defined by his past but by the grace that rescued him from it.

## Appearance

A man of middle years with a sharply twisted spine that forces a lurching, sideways gait — striking at first glance, quickly forgotten by anyone who looks at his face. His expression is calm and open in the way of someone who has stopped defending himself against the world's judgments.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Festival morning:** At the cathedral, not quite in the square — preparing for the consecration ceremony.
- **After the attack:** Tending to the boneyard and the cathedral. He discovered the theft of Father Tobyn's remains first — before even Father Zantus knew.
- **General:** In the boneyard (area 2) or the cathedral almost always. The bells ring on schedule every single day.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Friendly toward anyone who defends Sandpoint. Does not judge by appearance, having experienced a lifetime of judgment himself.
- **Warms when:** PCs treat him without flinching at his gait; when they show genuine respect for the boneyard or Father Tobyn's memory; when they share any connection to Sarenrae or redemption.
- **Cools when:** PCs disrespect the cemetery, mock his appearance, or press him too hard on subjects he has been asked to keep silent about without first earning his trust.
- **Voice and mannerism:** Quiet and deliberate. He chooses his words carefully — someone who once spoke carelessly and learned the cost. When asked about Tobyn his composure holds, but his hands go still.
- **Emphasise:** The weight he carries about the remains theft. He discovered it himself, reported it, and was told to stay silent. The silence is not comfortable for him — it sits against everything Sarenrae asks of him.
- **Avoid:** Making him seem furtive or untrustworthy. He is not hiding things out of self-interest. He is keeping a confidence out of obedience and it costs him something every day.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy (ask about Tobyn), DC 12:** He becomes emotional but shares his gratitude for Tobyn's kindness and what the priest meant to him. — unlocks: **Tobyn's remains stolen** (see Secrets).
- **Knowledge (Religion) re: Sarenrae, DC 10:** Common ground; he opens up significantly.
- **Sense Motive, DC 13:** He is deeply distressed about the remains theft but maintaining silence on Zantus's request. — unlocks: **Tobyn's remains stolen** (see Secrets).
- **Diplomacy (after full trust), DC 16:** He shares knowledge of the coastal smuggling routes from his past — unlocks: **Smuggling routes** (see Secrets).

## Secrets

- **Tobyn's remains stolen:** He discovered the theft before Father Zantus did — he does the dawn rounds and noticed the disturbed earth himself. He reported it to Zantus immediately and has been asked to keep quiet. The silence weighs on him. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 12 (cross-reference existing check) or Sense Motive DC 13 (cross-reference existing check)*
- **Smuggling routes:** From his past life he knows three concealed coastal landing points north of Sandpoint that smugglers used, including one accessible from the Nettlewood. He has never shared this with anyone because he is ashamed of who he was. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 16 after full trust is established, or after PCs learn of his past and accept him without judgment*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** Can provide burial rites, minor healing (Cleric 2), and information about who is buried in the boneyard. Knows the cemetery layout intimately — crucial if PCs investigate the grave theft. Can identify which plots are undisturbed and which have been interfered with.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs disrespect the cemetery or treat him with cruelty. Refuses access to the boneyard. Reports to Father Zantus.
- **If Killed:** The boneyard falls into disrepair. The cathedral bells go silent. Sandpoint notices the absence and mourns publicly — the bells have never missed a toll.

## Narrative Function

Holds the first thread of the Tobyn's-remains subplot — he is the one who noticed the theft before anyone else. His redemption arc (smuggler turned devout keeper) reinforces the campaign's themes of corruption and grace. The silencing of his bells is one of Sandpoint's most visceral signals that something has gone deeply wrong.
