# Brodert Quink

**Aliases:** brodert, quink, sage, scholar, old quink
**Locations:** old light, curious goblin, turandarok academy, area 8, quink's building

## Personality

Cantankerous, brilliant, and perpetually baffled that the world has not recognised his genius — he has spent years studying the Old Light and is absolutely convinced it was a Thassilonian war machine, a theory everyone else dismisses as eccentric. He is right. He is dismissive of fools and sycophants but responds with genuine enthusiasm to anyone who engages seriously with his ideas, and beneath the bluster is a loneliness born of being correct too early and too consistently.

## Appearance

An elderly man, slightly disheveled, who perpetually carries a battered notebook stuffed with loose measurements and annotated diagrams. He gestures expansively when excited, which is often.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **At the festival:** Present but standing apart from the crowd near the Old Light end of the square, probably complaining to anyone who'll listen.
- **After the attack:** At his building (area 8) or the Curious Goblin (area 22) arguing with Chask.
- **General:** Area 8 most days. Occasionally at Turandarok Academy arguing with Veznutt Parooh.

## GM Notes

- **Initial attitude:** Indifferent, bordering on dismissive. Attitude shifts to immediately engaged the moment Thassilon, the Old Light, or ancient ruins are mentioned.
- **Warms further:** PCs who engage seriously with his theories, bring him artifacts, or demonstrate genuine Knowledge (history) or Knowledge (arcana). Attitude jumps to Helpful on receipt of a Thassilonian artifact.
- **Cools:** Being mocked publicly or having his theories dismissed. Becomes an obstacle — may spread unflattering rumors.
- **Voice/mannerism:** Brusque, lecture-prone. Interrupts. Assumes the person he's talking to knows less than he does (usually correct). Softens slightly with PCs who prove him wrong about their intelligence.
- **Emphasise:** He is right about everything. His isolation is the cost of being correct too early. The loneliness beneath the bluster is real and not often seen.
- **Avoid:** Making him merely comic. His knowledge is genuinely invaluable and he knows it — the arrogance is earned.

## Social Checks

- **Knowledge (history):** DC 12 — knowing enough about Thassilon to engage him meaningfully improves attitude to Friendly
- **Knowledge (arcana):** DC 15 — engaging on magical theory of the Old Light; he becomes enthusiastic and forthcoming
- **Diplomacy (ask about the Old Light):** DC 10 — launches into a lengthy explanation of his war-machine theory
- **Diplomacy (bring Thassilonian artifact):** Automatic — attitude jumps immediately to Helpful — unlocks: Location of the Catacombs (see Secrets)
- **Sense Motive:** DC 13 — beneath the bluster he is lonely; his theories have isolated him socially
- **Knowledge (arcana) + Diplomacy (established trust):** DC 15 + DC 16 — unlocks: Runewell theory (see Secrets)

## Secrets

- **Location of the Catacombs:** His research into the Old Light has produced maps and measurements that suggest an underground complex beneath the northern part of town, accessible somewhere near the tannery district. He hasn't shared this because no one takes him seriously. — *Unlocked by: Diplomacy DC 14 after bringing him a Thassilonian artifact*
- **Runewell theory:** He has partially decoded a Thassilonian inscription that describes a "well of wrath" beneath the Old Light — a reservoir of magical energy tied to the sin of wrath. He believes it is still active. He thinks about it constantly. — *Unlocked by: Knowledge (arcana) DC 15 + Diplomacy DC 16 after establishing genuine trust*

## State Handling

- **If Cooperative:** A crucial lore resource. Can identify Thassilonian runes, artifacts, and history. Provides context for the runewell, the Catacombs of Wrath, and Karzoug's history. Can read partial Thassilonian texts. Will share everything freely once convinced the PCs are serious.
- **If Neutral:** Politely dismissive. Will answer basic factual questions.
- **If Distrustful:** PCs mock his theories publicly. Refuses to help. Becomes an obstacle — may spread unflattering rumors about them around town.
- **If Killed:** Sandpoint's primary Thassilonian scholar is gone. Identifying artifacts becomes significantly harder. Key lore about Karzoug and the Catacombs becomes inaccessible without magical assistance.

## Narrative Function

The campaign's primary lore bridge between the immediate goblin threat and the deeper Thassilonian conspiracy. His dismissal by polite Sandpoint society mirrors the PCs' own growing understanding that the Old Light, the runewell, and the Catacombs are real and dangerous. Bringing him an artifact is a natural milestone that unlocks lore and cements his usefulness.
