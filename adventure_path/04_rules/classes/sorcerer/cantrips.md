# Cantrips

**Type:** Sorcerer Class Ability  
**Source:** Pathfinder Core Rulebook, p.72  
**Gained at:** Level 1

---

## Description

> Sorcerers learn a number of cantrips, or 0-level spells. These spells are
> cast like any other spell, but they do not consume any slots and may be used
> again. Cantrips prepared using other spell slots, due to metamagic feats for
> example, consume spell slots as normally.

---

## Mechanical Details

- **Unlimited uses per day.** Cantrips never consume spell slots regardless of how many times they are cast.
- **Full spellcasting action economy.** Casting a cantrip is still a standard action and still provokes attacks of opportunity unless the caster has Eschew Materials or the spell has no material component.
- **Cantrips known scales with level.** A sorcerer knows a fixed number of 0-level spells chosen at character creation (and possibly expanded later). Unlike prepared casters, a sorcerer cannot "swap out" which cantrips are available day-to-day — the list is fixed until a level-up allows a swap.
- **Not subject to spell slot enhancement** except through metamagic, which does consume a higher slot.

## Example Cantrips (Level 1)

| Cantrip | School | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Detect Magic | Divination | Scan area for magical auras (concentration, up to 1 min, 60 ft cone) |
| Disrupt Undead | Evocation | Ranged touch, 1d6 positive energy vs. undead |
| Message | Transmutation | Whispered message to target up to 110 ft, 10 min duration |
| Read Magic | Divination | Decipher magical writing; personal, 10 min |
