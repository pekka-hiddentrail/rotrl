# Laughing Touch

**Type:** Sorcerer Bloodline Power (Fey Bloodline)  
**Source:** Pathfinder Core Rulebook, p.76  
**Gained at:** Level 1  
**Uses per day:** 3 + CHA modifier (8/day at level 1 with CHA 20)

---

## Description

> You can cause a creature to burst out laughing for 1 round as a melee touch
> attack. A laughing creature can only take a move action but can defend itself
> normally. Once a creature has been affected by laughing touch, it is immune
> to its effects for 24 hours. You can use this ability a number of times per
> day equal to 3 + your Charisma modifier. This is a mind-affecting effect.

---

## Mechanical Details

- **Action:** Melee touch attack (standard action, provokes AoO unless used as part of a charge or other qualifying action).
- **Attack roll:** Melee touch attack using STR modifier. A sorcerer with STR –1 and BAB +0 has a –1 touch attack bonus. Weapon Finesse can substitute DEX if the character has that feat.
- **Effect on hit:** Target can only take a **move action** for 1 round. It can still defend (AC applies, it may 5-foot step, etc.) but cannot attack, cast spells, or take standard/full-round actions.
- **No saving throw.** If the touch attack hits, the effect applies automatically. No Will save.
- **24-hour immunity per creature.** Once a creature has been affected, it cannot be affected again until the next day.
- **Mind-affecting.** Creatures immune to mind-affecting effects (constructs, undead, many outsiders) are immune.
- **Uses per day:** 3 + CHA mod (e.g., 3 + 5 = **8/day** at level 1 with CHA 20).
- **Does not scale** — the effect is always "1 round, move-action only." Only the uses/day count grows as CHA improves.

## Tactical Notes

- Extremely powerful action-denial at level 1. Stopping a caster or archer from taking a standard action for a round is equivalent to a staggered condition.
- Best used against the most dangerous enemy in the first round of combat, before they can act.
- Stacks poorly with the party trying to full-attack the same target (the laughing target can still move away). Best when combined with a terrain lock or the sorcerer staying adjacent.
- Cannot be used in the same turn as a spell requiring a standard action, since the touch attack is itself a standard action. Can be combined with a move.
