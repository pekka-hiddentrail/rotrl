# Blessings

**Type:** Warpriest Class Ability  
**Source:** Pathfinder Advanced Class Guide, p.60  
**Gained at:** Level 1  
**Uses per day:** 3 + WIS modifier (7/day at level 1 with WIS 18)  
**Save DC:** 10 + half warpriest level + WIS modifier (DC 14 at level 1)  
**Blessings:** Healing, Strength

---

## Description

> A warpriest can call upon the power of her deity to channel divine power into
> her blessings. Each blessing grants a minor ability at 1st level and a major
> ability at 10th level. A warpriest can call upon any of her blessings'
> abilities a number of times per day equal to 3 + her Wisdom modifier.

---

## Mechanical Details

- **Uses per day:** 3 + WIS = 3 + 4 = **7/day** at level 1. All blessings draw from this shared pool.
- **Save DC:** 10 + (level ÷ 2, rounded down) + WIS = 10 + 0 + 4 = **DC 14** at level 1.
- **Action to activate:** Unless noted otherwise, using a blessing is a **swift action**.
- **Blessing selection** is permanent — a warpriest selects two blessings at character creation, typically from the deity's favored domains. These cannot be changed.
- **Minor vs. Major abilities:** Only the minor (1st-level) abilities are available until level 10. See individual blessing files for full details.

## Blessings at Level 1 (Healing + Strength example)

| Blessing | Minor Ability | Uses Same Pool? |
|---|---|---|
| Healing | Powerful Healer — empower a cure spell as a swift action | Yes |
| Strength | Strength Surge — +1 enhancement bonus on STR-based rolls for 1 round | Yes |

See `powerful_healer.md` and `strength_surge.md` for full mechanical breakdowns.

## Blessing Progression by Warpriest Level

| Warpriest Level | Blessings Change |
|---|---|
| 1 | Minor blessing abilities available |
| 10 | Major blessing abilities unlock |

## Tactical Notes

- 7 uses/day is generous at level 1. The pool is shared, so decide each combat whether to bias toward offense (Strength Surge on attack turns) or defense/support (Powerful Healer when allies drop).
- Both minor blessings (Healing and Strength) are swift actions — they can be combined with a standard action attack or spell in the same turn with no action economy loss.
