# Powerful Healer (Healing Blessing — Minor)

**Type:** Warpriest Blessing Ability (Healing, Minor)  
**Source:** Pathfinder Advanced Class Guide, p.65  
**Gained at:** Level 1  
**Action:** Swift  
**Uses:** Draws from shared Blessings pool (see `blessings.md`)

---

## Description

> You can add power to a cure spell as you cast it. As a swift action, you can
> treat any cure spell as if it were empowered (as the Empower Spell feat),
> causing it to heal 50% more damage (or deal 50% more damage if used against
> undead). This ability doesn't stack with itself or the Empower Spell feat.

---

## Mechanical Details

- **Trigger:** Declare before casting a cure spell. The swift action is taken on the same turn as the cure spell (standard action for the spell itself).
- **Effect:** The cure spell heals (or damages undead) for 1.5× its normal result. All dice are rolled normally; the total is then multiplied by 1.5 and rounded down.
- **Does not consume a higher spell slot.** Unlike the Empower Spell metamagic feat, this empowerment costs a use of the Blessings pool, not a +2 spell level adjustment.
- **Does not stack with Empower Spell.** If the warpriest later acquires the Empower Spell feat and prepares an empowered cure, Powerful Healer cannot stack on top of it.
- **Does not stack with itself.** Cannot be activated twice on the same casting.
- **Applies to all cure spells the warpriest knows.** Currently Cure Light Wounds (when prepared). The effect scales — Cure Moderate Wounds, Cure Serious Wounds, etc. all benefit equally.

## Example at Level 1

Cure Light Wounds normally heals 1d8 + caster level = 1d8+1.  
With Powerful Healer: (1d8+1) × 1.5 → average 6.75 → up to **10 HP** healed on a good roll, versus 6 HP average base.

## Tactical Notes

- Use when a single heal needs to restore the most possible HP in one action — e.g., an ally at 3 HP who will die from another hit.
- Does not require the warpriest to be the one in danger; any adjacent ally can be healed with a touch.
- Swift + Standard pairing means the warpriest can still attack or cast in the same round.
