# Eschew Materials

**Type:** General Feat  
**Source:** Pathfinder Core Rulebook, p.123  
**Prerequisites:** None

---

## Description

> You can cast many spells without needing to utilize minor material components.
> You can cast any spell with a material component costing 1 gp or less without
> needing that component. The casting of the spell still provokes attacks of
> opportunity as normal. If the spell requires a material component that costs
> more than 1 gp, you must have the material component on hand to cast the
> spell, as normal.

---

## Mechanical Advantage

- **Removes pouch dependency for cheap components.** Spells that list a material
  component (bat guano, fur, a pinch of sand, etc.) with a cost of 1 gp or less
  can be cast without producing or consuming that component.
- **Provocation is unchanged.** Casting still provokes attacks of opportunity;
  this feat provides no defensive benefit.
- **Does not remove costly material components.** Anything listed as costing
  more than 1 gp — diamond dust (Stoneskin), a ruby (Contingency), etc. — must
  still be physically present and is consumed on casting.
- **Focus and Divine Focus items are unaffected** — those are a separate category
  and are never removed by this feat.
