# Swift Action

**Triggers:** swift action, quickened, quickened spell, swift spell, swift cast

## Rules

A swift action consumes a very small amount of time but represents more effort than a free action.

**Limit: one swift action per turn**, regardless of any other actions taken. You can take a swift action any time you would normally be allowed to take a free action.

Swift actions are still permitted during a full-round action.

Common uses: spellcasting (quickened spells, free/swift casting time spells), activating feats, activating magic items.

## Cast a Quickened Spell

Cast a spell modified by **Quicken Spell**, or any spell with a casting time of "free action" or "swift action", as a swift action. Does **not** provoke AoO.

Only one such spell may be cast per round. It does not count against your normal limit of one spell per round — you may cast a regular spell and a quickened spell in the same round.

## Other Common Swift Actions

**Activate certain class abilities:** class features that specify "swift action" — swift hexes, judgments, lay on hands (swift via Mercy feat), etc.

**Activate certain magic items:** items whose activation is designated as a swift action.

<!-- REFERENCE -->

If you use an immediate action during someone else's turn, it counts as your swift action for your next round — you cannot take both a swift action and an immediate action in the same round.
