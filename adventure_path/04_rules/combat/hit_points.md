# Hit Points

**Triggers:** hit points, hp, damage, dying, unconscious, dead, death, stabilize, stable, healing, healed, negative hp, coup de grace, nonlethal, lethal, fall unconscious, drops to zero

## HP Thresholds

| HP | State |
|---|---|
| > 0 | Active — can act normally |
| 0 | Disabled — can take one move or standard action; any strenuous action deals 1 damage |
| −1 to −(CON−1) | Dying — unconscious, loses 1 HP/round unless stabilised |
| −CON or lower | Dead |

## Stabilising

Each round at negative HP: DC 10 + damage below 0 CON check to stabilise.
Ally can stabilise with DC 15 Heal check (standard action). Stabilised: no longer loses HP; remains unconscious until healed above 0.

## Damage Reduction (DR)

Written as `DR N/type` (e.g. DR 5/silver). Subtract N from each hit of the matching type; 0 minimum. Bypassed by weapon with correct material or alignment.

## Nonlethal Damage

Tracked separately. When nonlethal ≥ current HP, creature is staggered (nonlethal = HP) or unconscious (nonlethal > HP). Heals at 1/hour (or with magic).

## Healing

- Natural: 1 HP/level per night of rest; 2× with complete bed rest
- Cure spells: heal HP equal to spell result
- Fast healing / regeneration: HP per round (regeneration regrows limbs)

## Massive Damage

Optional rule (not used by default): if a single hit deals ≥ 50 damage, DC 15 Fort save or die.

## Death Effects

Some spells or abilities kill outright, bypassing HP. Fort save allowed unless stated otherwise.
