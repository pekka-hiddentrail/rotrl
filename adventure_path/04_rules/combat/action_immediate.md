# Immediate Action

**Triggers:** immediate action, immediate, off turn, not my turn, react, feather fall

## Rules

An immediate action is like a swift action in effort, but can be taken **at any time — even on another creature's turn**.

**On your own turn:** counts as your swift action for that turn. You cannot also use a swift action.

**Off your turn:** costs your swift action for your *coming* turn. You cannot use another immediate action or a swift action until after your next turn.

**Flat-footed:** you cannot use an immediate action while flat-footed.

## Example

*Feather fall* — cast as an immediate action the moment you begin falling, before damage would be applied.

<!-- REFERENCE -->

Immediate actions do not have their own initiative count — they interrupt the normal sequence and resolve before the triggering event continues.
