# Full-Round Action

**Triggers:** full round, full-round, full attack, charge, run, withdraw, coup de grace, iterative attacks, metamagic, difficult terrain, fighting defensively, two weapons, two-weapon

## Rules

A full-round action requires the entire round. Cannot be combined with a standard or move action. If the action involves no movement, you may take a 5-foot step (before, during, or after). Some full-round actions do not allow a 5-foot step — check the specific action.

Free actions and swift actions are still permitted alongside a full-round action.

**Restricted activity:** if limited to a single standard or move action (surprise round, etc.), full-round actions are not available, though you may start or complete one using a standard action (see **Start/complete a full-round action** in [action_standard.md](action_standard.md)). Cannot be used for full attack, charge, run, or withdraw.

## Full-Round Actions

### Full Attack

Required whenever you want more than one attack in a round (high BAB iterative attacks, two-weapon fighting, double weapons, or other special sources).

- Iterative attacks (high BAB): must be made highest bonus to lowest.
- Two weapons or double weapon: either weapon/part may attack first.
- Only movement allowed: one 5-foot step (before, after, or between attacks).
- You do not need to declare all targets in advance — resolve earlier attacks before assigning later ones.

**Switching to a move after the first attack:** after your first attack in a full attack, you may abandon the remaining attacks and take a move action instead — but only if you have not already taken a move action this round. If you have already taken a 5-foot step, you cannot use that move action for actual movement, but you can use a different kind of move action.

**Fighting defensively (full attack):** −4 to all attacks this round; +2 dodge bonus to AC until the start of your next turn.

### Cast a Spell (Full-Round)

A spell with a 1-round casting time: begins on your turn, takes effect just before your turn in the following round. You act normally after the spell completes.

A spell with a 1-minute casting time: takes 10 consecutive, uninterrupted full-round actions. If concentration is broken at any point, the spell is lost.

AoO is provoked only when you **begin** casting, not on subsequent rounds. While casting, you do not threaten any squares.

**Metamagic (sorcerers and bards only):** casting a metamagic version of a standard-action spell is a full-round action (except Quicken Spell, which is a swift action). The spell takes effect the same round you begin casting — it is not a 1-round casting time. For spells already requiring a full round or longer, add one full-round action.

**Clerics and druids (spontaneous metamagic):** spontaneously casting a metamagic cure, inflict, or summon spell with a normal casting time of 1 standard action becomes a full-round action. Longer casting times add one full-round action.

See [action_spellcasting.md](action_spellcasting.md) for concentration, defensive casting, and component rules.

### Run

Move up to 4× speed in a straight line (3× in heavy armor). No 5-foot step permitted. Lose Dex bonus to AC unless you have the Run feat.

You can run for a number of rounds equal to your Constitution score. After that, make a DC 10 Con check each round to continue; DC increases by 1 per check. Failure means you must stop. After running to your limit, rest 1 minute (10 rounds) before running again — no faster than a normal move action during rest.

Cannot run across difficult terrain or when you cannot see where you are going.

### Withdraw

Move up to **double your speed**. The square you start in is not threatened — visible enemies do not get AoO when you leave it. Invisible enemies still get AoO; you cannot withdraw while blinded. No 5-foot step on the same round.

If you move through any other threatened square during the withdrawal, those enemies get AoO as normal.

You may not withdraw using a movement type for which you have no listed speed.

You do not have to leave combat entirely — the name is not literal.

**Restricted withdraw:** if limited to a standard action only, you may withdraw as a standard action, moving up to your speed (not double).

### Move 5 Feet through Difficult Terrain

When your speed is so hampered you cannot even take a 5-foot step, spend a full-round action to move 5 ft in any direction. This is **not** a 5-foot step — it provokes AoO normally.

### Coup de Grace

Full-round action against a helpless adjacent foe. Automatically a critical hit; target must make a Fortitude save (DC = 10 + damage dealt) or die. Provokes AoO.

### Use Special Ability (Full-Round)

Some special abilities specify a full-round action. See the ability's description. For Sp/Su/Ex rules see [action_standard.md](action_standard.md).

### Charge

Move up to 2× speed in a straight unobstructed line toward an enemy, then make one melee attack at the end. +2 attack roll, −2 AC until the start of your next turn. Requires a clear, unobstructed path to the target.
