# Standard Action

**Triggers:** standard action, attack, activate, aid another, can i attack, draw weapon, sheathe, unarmed, ranged, natural attack, critical hit, crit, threat, fighting defensively, melee, shoot into melee, stabilize, feint, channel energy, read a scroll, escape grapple

## Rules

The most common action type. Allows you to do something meaningful — most commonly make an attack or cast a spell.

A standard action can be used as a move action (not vice versa).

In a normal round you get one standard action and one move action. In a surprise round or other restricted situation you may be limited to one standard action OR one move action total.

## Attack

One melee or ranged attack at highest attack bonus. For multiple attacks per round use a full attack (see [action_full_round.md](action_full_round.md)).

**Melee:** any opponent within 5 ft (adjacent). Reach weapons hit at 10 ft but cannot hit adjacent foes.

**Ranged:** any target in line of sight within maximum range (thrown: 5 range increments; projectile: 10 range increments). Provokes AoO.

**Shooting or throwing into melee:** −4 penalty if a friendly character is engaged in melee with your target (i.e. both threaten each other). Reduced to −2 if target is two size categories larger than the friendly; no penalty at three or more. No penalty with **Precise Shot** feat.

### Unarmed Attacks
Provokes AoO from the target if that target is armed. Does not provoke from other foes or from unarmed targets.

An unarmed character cannot make AoOs.

**"Armed" unarmed:** monks, Improved Unarmed Strike feat holders, spellcasters delivering touch attacks, and creatures with natural weapons count as armed for both offense and defense.

**Damage:** Medium 1d3 bludgeoning + Str; Small 1d2; Large 1d4. All nonlethal unless you take −4 on the attack roll to deal lethal. With Improved Unarmed Strike, no penalty for lethal.

Unarmed strikes count as light weapons (two-weapon penalty purposes).

### Natural Attacks
Melee attacks against any creature within reach. Use full attack bonus; damage by type + Str mod.

No extra attacks for high BAB — extra attacks come from additional limbs/body parts.

**Single natural attack only:** add 1½ × Str mod to damage.

**Secondary natural attacks** (tails, wings): made at BAB −5; add only ½ Str mod to damage.

**Mixing with manufactured weapons:** if you combine natural attacks with weapon attacks in the same full attack, all natural attacks become secondary (BAB −5, ½ Str). Two-Weapon Fighting and Multiattack can reduce penalties.

### Critical Hits
Natural 20 on the attack roll = threat regardless of AC. Immediately roll to **confirm**: repeat the attack roll with the same modifiers. If the confirmation hits the target's AC, it's a critical hit.

**Critical hit damage:** roll all damage dice twice (including static bonuses), add together. Precision damage (sneak attack) and extra dice from weapon abilities (e.g. *flaming*) are **not** multiplied.

**Default:** threat on 20, multiplier ×2. Many weapons have expanded threat ranges or higher multipliers.

**Expanded threat range:** a roll below 20 in the expanded range is still not an automatic hit — if the attack misses AC, it is not a threat.

**Spells:** spells requiring an attack roll can crit. Spells with no attack roll cannot. Ability damage/drain from spells is doubled on a crit.

### Fighting Defensively (Standard Action)
−4 penalty on all attacks this round; gain +2 dodge bonus to AC until the start of your next turn.

## Other Common Standard Actions

**Cast a spell (1 standard action):** takes effect immediately. Provokes AoO. See [action_spellcasting.md](action_spellcasting.md).

**Activate a magic item:** standard action unless the item says otherwise.

- **Spell completion items** (scrolls): equivalent to casting a spell — requires concentration, provokes AoO. Concentration broken = spell lost. Can cast defensively (see [action_spellcasting.md](action_spellcasting.md)).
- **Spell trigger items** (wands, staves): no concentration, no AoO.
- **Command word items:** no concentration, no AoO.
- **Use-activated items:** no concentration, no AoO.

Many magic items (passive bonuses, worn items) need no activation at all.

**Drink a potion / apply an oil:** provokes AoO.

**Read a scroll:** provokes AoO.

**Channel energy:** does not provoke.

**Aid another:** make an attack roll DC 10 against AC 10. On success, grant one ally +2 on their next attack roll or +2 AC until your next turn. Provokes only if you move into a threatened square.

**Total defense:** +4 dodge bonus to AC until your next turn. AC improves at the start of the action. Does not provoke AoO. Cannot be combined with fighting defensively or Combat Expertise. You cannot make AoOs while using total defense.

**Feint:** Bluff check opposed by target's Bluff or Sense Motive (their choice). On success, target loses Dex bonus to AC against your next attack this round (or next round if the target acts before you). Does not provoke.

**Escape a grapple:** one combat maneuver check or Escape Artist check to break free. Does not provoke.

**Stabilize a dying friend:** Heal check DC 15. On success, the target stops dying. Provokes AoO.

**Ready:** declare a standard action and a trigger. Your action fires when the trigger occurs (interrupts like an immediate action). Loses your next initiative step.

**Start/complete a full-round action:** use one standard action to begin a full-round action this round, then another standard action next round to finish it. Cannot be used for full attack, charge, run, or withdraw.

**Use a spell-like ability (Sp):** standard action (unless otherwise noted). Requires concentration; provokes AoO; can be disrupted. If broken, the attempt fails but still counts as having been used. Can be used defensively: Concentration check DC 15 + double spell level; failure means the ability is not used but is still expended.

**Use a supernatural ability (Su):** standard action (unless otherwise noted). Cannot be disrupted, no concentration, no AoO.

**Use an extraordinary ability (Ex):** usually not an action (reactive/automatic). When it is an action, typically a standard action. Cannot be disrupted, no concentration, no AoO.
