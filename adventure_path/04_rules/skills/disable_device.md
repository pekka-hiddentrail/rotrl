# Disable Device

**Triggers:** disable device, disable trap, disarm trap, pick lock, open lock, unlock, bypass lock, jam mechanism, sabotage, check for traps, detect trap, disarm, defuse, lockpick, get this door open, get past the lock

## Resolution

**DC check using Dexterity modifier + Disable Device ranks. Armor check penalty applies. Requires training.**

The check is **made secretly** — the character does not necessarily know whether they succeeded.

### Device Complexity

| Complexity | Time | DC | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Simple | 1 round | 10 | Jam a lock |
| Tricky | 1d4 rounds | 15 | Sabotage a wagon wheel |
| Difficult | 2d4 rounds | 20 | Disarm a trap, reset a trap |
| Extreme | 2d4 rounds | 25 | Disarm a complex trap, sabotage a clockwork device |

Add **+5 to DC** if you want to leave no trace of tampering.

**Fail by 4 or less:** Failed but can retry.  
**Fail by 5+:** Something goes wrong. On a trap: it triggers. On sabotage: you believe the device is disabled but it still functions normally.

You can also rig a simple device to work normally for a while and then fail or fall off (usually after 1d4 rounds or minutes of use).

### Open Locks

| Lock Quality | DC |
|---|---|
| Simple | 20 |
| Average | 25 |
| Good | 30 |
| Superior | 40 |

Without thieves’ tools: **+10 to all lock DCs**. Masterwork thieves’ tools: +2 circumstance bonus.

## GM Notes

- **Rolls are secret** — do not tell the player whether they succeeded until the consequence is visible. This applies especially to sabotage: on a fail by 5+, tell the player the device looks disabled. The GM tracks the truth.
- **Fail by 4 or less = retry** — players can try again on traps and locks after a near-miss; only a bad fail (5+) triggers the trap or locks them out.
- **Trapfinding required for magic traps** — only rogues (and classes with trapfinding) can disarm magical traps. DC = 25 + spell level. Non-rogues simply cannot attempt it.
- **Sandpoint Glassworks** — storage basement has a simple padlock (DC 20); hidden tunnel entrance is an average lock (DC 25). Neither is trapped.
- **Catacombs of Wrath** — one difficult mechanical trap (DC 20) and one glyph of warding (magical; trapfinding + DC 25 + spell level). Always call for Perception first to detect before Disable Device to disarm.
- **Thistletop dungeon level** — good locks (DC 30) on key chambers; one extreme mechanical trap (DC 25) in the main corridor. Players without a rogue will need keys or brute force (break DC on a good lock is typically Str 28).

## What It Does

Disable Device covers disarming traps, picking locks, sabotaging mechanical devices, and rigging devices to fail on a delay. It can also be used to jam a lock so it cannot be opened, or to reset a disarmed trap.

## Untrained

Cannot be attempted untrained. Requires at least 1 rank (training).

## Action

Varies. Disabling a simple device (DC 10) takes 1 round and is a full-round action. Tricky devices take 1d4 rounds; difficult and extreme devices take 2d4 rounds. Opening a lock is a full-round action.

## Try Again

Varies. You can retry a failed trap-disarm or lock-open check if you failed by **4 or less**. If you fail by 5 or more on a trap, it triggers and cannot be retried. If you fail by 5 or more on sabotage, you believe you succeeded — the device still functions normally.

## Special

- The **Deft Hands** feat grants a bonus on Disable Device checks.
- Masterwork thieves’ tools grant a +2 circumstance bonus.
- Noticing a trap requires a Perception check, not Disable Device.

## Restriction

Only characters with the **trapfinding** class feature (rogues and certain others) can disarm magical traps. A magical trap’s DC is **25 + the spell level** used to create it. The spells *fire trap*, *glyph of warding*, *symbol*, and *teleportation circle* create traps that a rogue can disarm with Disable Device. *Spike growth* and *spike stones* create magic hazards that **cannot** be disarmed with this skill.

A rogue who beats a trap’s DC by 10 or more can study it well enough to **bypass it without disarming** it and can rig it so allies may also bypass it.
