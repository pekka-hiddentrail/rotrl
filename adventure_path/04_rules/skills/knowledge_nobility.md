# Knowledge (Nobility)

**Triggers:** knowledge nobility, noble, nobility, heraldry, lineage, etiquette, royalty, ruling family, ruling house, line of succession, who rules, who is in charge, lord, lady, noble house, noble family, coat of arms, herald, crest, proper address, how do i address, how should i address, how do we greet, title, who is this person, aristocrat, aristocracy, magnimar, baron, count, lord-mayor

## Resolution

**DC check using INT modifier + Knowledge (Nobility) ranks.** Untrained characters (0 ranks) can attempt DC 10 checks only.

| DC | Information Type |
|---|---|
| 10 | Know current rulers and their symbols |
| 15 | Know proper etiquette for a given social context |
| 20 | Know line of succession; obscure noble houses and their politics |
| 25 | Hidden family connections, illegitimate lines, suppressed history of a noble house |

## GM Notes

- Nobility is rarely triggered in Act I Sandpoint — the town is frontier, not court
- It becomes relevant when PCs interact with Aldern Foxglove (minor Magnimarian socialite, can be identified by crest/manner), Lonjiku Kaijitsu (Minkaian noble lineage), or when the party travels to Magnimar
- Failed checks: the PC simply doesn't know; don't confabulate false noble lines
- A PC with a noble background (if any) should get +2 circumstance on checks about their own nation or region

## What It Does

Knowledge (Nobility) covers lineages, heraldry, personalities, and royalty. In this campaign it is most relevant when dealing with Magnimarian social hierarchies and the founding families of Sandpoint.

## Untrained

Can be attempted untrained for DC 10 or lower. Access to a genealogical library removes the DC 10 limit but increases check time to 1d4 hours.

## Action

Usually none. Recalling knowledge is mental and instantaneous.

## Try Again

No. The check represents what you know — thinking about a topic a second time doesn't let you know something you never learned.

## Special

A bard adds half class level (minimum 1) on all Knowledge checks, even untrained.
Characters of noble birth gain a +2 circumstance bonus on Nobility checks related to their own nation or region.
