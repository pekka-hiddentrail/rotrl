# Stealth

**Triggers:** stealth, sneak, sneaking, hide, hiding, move silently, move quietly, slip past, slip by, creep, creeping, stay hidden, stay out of sight, avoid being seen, avoid detection, tail someone, follow without being noticed, shadow, shadows, lurk, lurking, approach without being heard, get closer without being noticed, blend in, conceal myself, conceal ourselves

## Resolution

**Opposed check: Stealth vs observer's Perception.**

The Stealth check is made once when the character attempts to hide or move silently. The observer's Perception is checked against it.

Creatures that fail to beat your Stealth check are not aware of you and **treat you as if you had total concealment**.

| Speed | Stealth Penalty |
|---|---|
| Up to half normal speed | None |
| Greater than half but less than full speed | −5 |
| Running or charging | Cannot use Stealth |

## Cover and Concealment

Stealth requires either cover or concealment to begin hiding. A character cannot hide in the open with no visual obstruction.

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| At least 50% concealment or soft cover | Required to attempt Stealth |
| Greater invisibility | May always attempt Stealth |
| Invisible and immobile | +40 bonus to Stealth |
| Invisible and moving | +20 bonus to Stealth |
| Dim light | +2 bonus |
| Darkness | +10 bonus; observer must succeed on Perception DC 0 just to be aware of presence |
| No cover or concealment | Cannot attempt |

## Breaking Stealth

When you start your turn using Stealth, you can leave cover or concealment and remain unobserved as long as you succeed at a Stealth check and **end your turn in cover or concealment**. Stealth immediately ends after you make an attack roll, whether or not the attack is successful (except when sniping — see below).

If an observer actively searches (Perception as a move action): opposed check regardless of whether the hider is moving.

## Sniping

If you have already successfully used Stealth at least 10 feet from your target, you can make one ranged attack and then **immediately use Stealth again as a move action**. You take a **−20 penalty** on the Stealth check to maintain your obscured location.

## Creating a Diversion to Hide

You can use Bluff to get the momentary diversion needed to attempt Stealth while people are aware of you. Make a Bluff check opposed by the viewer's **Sense Motive**. On success, you may immediately attempt a Stealth check — but at a **−10 penalty** because you have to move fast. You must reach an unobserved place of some kind before the end of that move.

## GM Notes

- Goblin ambush (Act I): goblins roll Stealth before the attack wave; PCs with high Perception may notice them early and avoid surprise
- Thistletop infiltration: Stealth vs goblin sentries' Perception; dim torchlight gives +2 to sneaking PCs; open courtyards give no cover (cannot attempt)
- Surprise round: if all opponents are successfully hidden when combat begins, they get a surprise round. If only some are detected, those opponents do not get a surprise round against the detecting PC
- Distraction: a loud noise or visible ally can give a Stealth checker a +2 circumstance bonus (GM discretion)
- Goblins have +6 Stealth (Small size +4, racial +2 Stealth) — they are reliably sneaky in undergrowth

## What It Does

Stealth allows a character to conceal their presence from observers. It covers moving silently and staying visually hidden. It is opposed by the observer's Perception.

## Armor Check Penalty

Stealth takes the full armor check penalty.

## Action

Usually none — Stealth is made as part of movement. Using Stealth immediately after a ranged attack (Sniping) is a **move action**. The Bluff check to create a diversion is a standard action (separate from the Stealth check itself).

## Try Again

Generally yes — each new attempt to move or hide is its own check.

## Special

A spellcaster with a cat familiar gets +3 on Stealth checks.
Rangers in their favored terrain add their favored terrain bonus to Stealth (and Perception) in that terrain.
Halflings and gnomes gain a +4 racial bonus on Stealth.
Rogues with Stealth as a class skill add class level rather than half class level for their trained bonus.
