# Ameiko Kaijitsu

**Tier:** II — Emotional Anchor
**Role:** Innkeeper of the Rusty Dragon; former adventurer
**Flags:** EMOTIONAL_ANCHOR, BRIDGE_NPC, PERSISTENT
**Aliases:** ameiko, kaijitsu, innkeeper, rusty dragon, ameiko kaijitsu
**Locations:** rusty dragon, inn, tavern, the rusty dragon

## Personality

Warm, sharp, and quietly restless. Ameiko gave up adventuring but never fully settled into the life of an innkeeper — she still carries herself like someone who expects trouble and knows how to handle it. She is perceptive, funny when relaxed, and guarded about her family. She does not pry, but she notices everything.

## Narrative Function

Humanises Sandpoint and gives the PCs a home base with a human face. She is the social heartbeat of the town — where people gather, gossip, and feel safe. Her arc connects to her troubled family (Lonjiku, Tsuto) and becomes significant from Act II onward.

## Location & Availability — Act I

- **Festival morning:** At the Rusty Dragon; may attend the ceremony later
- **During goblin attack:** Protecting customers at the Rusty Dragon or helping the wounded
- **After the attack:** Rusty Dragon (default) or Glassworks if family concern surfaces
- **General routine:** Runs the inn from morning through evening; closes after dark

## Reaction to PCs

Open and friendly from the start. Offers food, drink, a room, and rumours freely. Becomes genuinely fond of PCs who treat her as a person rather than a resource. Pulls back if she senses she is being used or if someone pushes too hard on the topic of her family.

## Social Checks

- **Diplomacy:** DC 8 for general goodwill and rumours; she is naturally cooperative
- **Gather Information:** Treat as DC 10 when asking through Ameiko; she knows most of Sandpoint
- **Bluff:** DC 16 — she has heard every traveller's story; hard but not impossible

## State Handling

- **If Friendly:** Informal aid, free meals, rumours, a safe place to plan; emotional investment in the party
- **If Alienated:** Social warmth fades; the Rusty Dragon becomes just a tavern; town connections dry up
- **If Killed:** Town loses its emotional centre; celebration and levity do not return; Ameiko's arc in later books collapses

## Notes

Her relationship with her father Lonjiku is strained. Do not reveal details of the Glassworks situation unless PCs investigate directly.
