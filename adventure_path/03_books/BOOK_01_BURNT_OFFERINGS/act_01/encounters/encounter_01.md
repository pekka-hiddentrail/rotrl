# Encounter 01 - Goblin Cavalry Charge (Swallowtail Festival)

## Encounter Summary

**Type:** Combat with social/tactical complications  
**Location:** Swallowtail Festival Grounds  
**Triggers:** Early-to-mid afternoon on festival day (timing flexible based on PC actions)  
**Challenge Rating:** Moderate for levels 1-3  
**XP Reward:** 600 XP (assuming 4 PCs level 1)  

---

## Narrative Setup

A sudden horn blast echoes across the festival. Red-scaled goblins on dire wolf mounts erupt from the forest edge, charging directly into the festival crowd. Screams erupt. The Sheriff immediately mobilizes militia. This is the campaign's first combat encounter.

---

## Encounter Objective

**Primary:** Protect civilians and stop/route the goblin attackers  
**Secondary:** Minimize civilian casualties (affects Pressure track)  
**Tertiary:** Capture/interrogate a goblin if possible (enables later Act II investigation)  

---

## Combatants: Goblin Forces

### Goblin Leader (Dire Wolf Mount)
**Stat Block:** Chief Goblin Rider  
- **HP:** 18 (dire wolf 18 + goblin 8)--note they are separate
- **AC:** 15 (leather, shield)
- **Attacks:** Scimitar +4 (1d6+2) or dire wolf bite (1d8+2)
- **Special:** Rides dire wolf (mobility, advantage on charge)
- **Tactics:** Leads with aggression; flees if reduced below 5 HP
- **CR:** 1

### Goblin Mounted Archers (x3)
**Stat Block:** Goblin Archer on Dire Wolf  
- **HP:** 12 each (dire wolf 12 + goblin 5)
- **AC:** 14 (leather)
- **Attacks:** Composite shortbow +3 (1d6+1, range 70 ft) or scimitar +3 (1d6+1)
- **Tactics:** Stay 40-60 feet back, rain arrows; flake if threatened in melee
- **CR:** 1 each

### Goblin Infantry (x4-6, variable based on difficulty)
**Stat Block:** Goblin Warrior  
- **HP:** 5 each
- **AC:** 15 (leather, shield)
- **Attacks:** Scimitar +2 (1d6+1) or shortbow +3 (1d6)
- **Morale:** Flee if 3+ of their number are killed
- **CR:** 1/4 each

**Total Estimated CR (4-person party):** 4 (Moderate encounter; CR ~1000 XP)

---

## Battlefield Layout

**Festival Grounds: ~60x80 feet cleared arena space**

```
        N (Forest)
        
    [Forest Edge]
    [Goblin spawn zone - 40 ft away]
    
OOOO    (Crowd of civilians in bleachers/standing)

## #### ## (Merchant tents/stalls - provide light cover)

        [Stage] (Mayor/VIPs present)

        S (Town entrance, Sheriff & militia 30 ft away)
```

**Key Terrain:**
- **Merchant tents:** Provide cover (AC +2) but are flammable/destructible
- **Bleachers:** Civilians seated; goblins will attack if unprotected
- **Open ground:** 40-ft run-in for goblins; disadvantageous for defenders without ranged attacks
- **Town buildings:** 30 ft south; militia begins arriving turn 2

---

## Combat Phases

### Round 1: Goblin Charge (Surprise Round)

**Setup:**
* Goblins charge into the festival grounds at 60 ft/round
* Goblins make Perception vs. PC Stealth: most PCs are surprised (not expecting combat)
* Goblins attack on "their first turn" (they have surprise)

**Goblin Actions (Surprise):**
- Dire wolf mounted leader charges closest target
- Archers loose arrows at crowd (or at any PC visible)
- Infantry follows 10 ft behind, ready to engage

**PC Options (if not surprised):**
* Ready actions (Readied attack if goblin moves into range)
* Ranged attacks (If PC has bow/spell at range 60 ft+)
* Movement (Run toward goblins or defend civilians)

**Civilian Impact:**
* **Without PC intervention:** 1d4 civilians take 1d6 arrow damage (none die unless critically hit)
* **With PC intervention:** PCs can block arrows (AC check) or warn crowd (Charisma/Perform check reduces damage)

---

### Round 2+: Full Combat

**Initiative:** Roll initiative after surprise round  

**Key Mechanical Points:**

| Action | Mechanics | Effect |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Targeting goblins | Attack roll vs. AC 14-15 | Standard combat resolution |
| Protecting civilians | Interpose your position OR warn them to flee | Reduces casualties |
| Flanking | Achieve during melee | +2 bonus to attack rolls |
| Area attacks (spells) | Hit all creatures in area (including civilians if in range) | Collateral damage likely |

**Goblin Morale:**
- 1st casualty: Goblins continue attacking
- 3rd+ casualty: Check morale (DC 10 Wisdom check for goblin leader)
  - Failure: Goblins rout (flee north back into forest)
  - Success: Goblins hold or escalate

**Sheriff & Militia Arrival:**
- **Round 2-3:** Sheriff Hemlock and 3-4 militia arrive as reinforcements
- **Effect:** Goblins are now outnumbered heavily; morale check becomes harder
- **Note:** Militia handle their own combats; PCs are primary heroes

---

## Encounter Tactics (Goblin AI)

**Leader's Goal:** Chaos and intimidation; if leader dies, goblins flee

**Archers' Goal:** Soft targets (civilians, spellcasters); retreat if flanked

**Infantry's Goal:** Close-range combat; protect ranged units

**Escape Plan:** If more than half numbers lost, flee north back to forest

**No Suicide:** Goblins won't stay and die; will route if clearly losing

---

## Environmental Hazards

* **Fire (if area is set ablaze):** Treat tents/wooden structures as catch fire (DC 12); deal 1d6 fire damage to adjacent creatures per round
* **Crowd panic:** Large spells (fireball, etc.) risk causing panic; saving throw DC 12 to avoid fleeing

---

## Treasure & Loot

**Goblin Equipment (Standard):**
- Scimitars (value: 15 gp each)
- Shortbows (value: 30 gp each)  
- Leather armor (value: 10 gp each)
- Dire wolf mounts (NOT lootable in combat; flee with riders if goblins rout)

**Total Potential Loot:** ~200-300 gp (modest for level 1)

**Captured Goblin (if alive):**
- Interrogation DC 12 (Intimidate or Diplomacy/Charisma)
- Provides info: "We camp at Thistletop. War Chief Ripnugget wants tribute, grrr!"
- Advancement to Act II investigation hook

---

## Experience Award

**Combat Victory (goblins routed/killed):** 600 XP total  
- Divide by number of PCs; ~150 XP per PC if party of 4

**Bonus XP:**
- Capturing goblin alive: +50 XP
- Killing leader: +25 XP
- Protecting named NPC: +50 XP each

---

## Consequences & Outcomes

### If Goblins Routed Quickly
- **Pressure +1** (relief, grateful townspeople)
- NPCs view PCs as heroes
- Investigation into goblin origins begins

### If High Civilian Casualties (5+)
- **Pressure +2** (shock, fear of return raids)
- Grieving families may approach PCs
- Economic fallout (reduced trade if people flee)

### If Goblins Escape (more than half numbers)
- **Pressure +2** (fear of retaliation)
- Future raids will be more coordinated
- Sheriff will seek PC help for subsequent defense

### If Leader Captured
- Enables interrogation for Act II hooks
- Goblins lose coordination in future encounters

---

## GM Notes

* **Tone:** First real combat; establish that actions matter and consequences are real
* **Pacing:** Don't stretch combat over multiple sessions; resolve in 1-2 sessions max
* **Drama:** Describe the chaos vividlyâ€”music stopping, people screaming, blood on cobblestones
* **NPC Deaths:** Avoid killing named NPCs unless PCs massively fail; killing background civilians is acceptable
* **Aftermath:** Use this encounter to establish PC heroes in town; NPCs will remember who fought hardest
