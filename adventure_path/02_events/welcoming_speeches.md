## Scene

The square before the new cathedral is busy well before the ceremony begins. Merchant tents line the edges selling food, cloth, local crafts, and painted wooden souvenirs shaped like swallowtail butterflies. The smell of fry-bread and roasting meat drifts from the far end. Children are already running. 

A wooden platform has been set up at the cathedral steps. Four speakers are scheduled. The crowd presses toward it with easy anticipation — this is a celebration, not a civic duty, and everyone present chose to be here.

## Beats

The speeches run in sequence. Each beat has a brief description, the full speech, and PC interaction notes. The LLM should narrate each speaker's delivery before checking for PC action.

---

### Beat 1 — Mayor Kendra Deverin

Mayor Deverin ascends the platform to warm applause. She is dressed practically for a mayor — good wool, a civic sash, no crown. She looks pleased in a genuine, unperformed way.

**Full speech:**

> "Sandpoint. Look at all of you. I want everyone to take a moment and look around — look at the people standing next to you, people you've known for years, people who drove two days to get here, people who've never seen our coast before today. *This* is what Sandpoint is.
>
> Five years ago, we lost something. We buried our dead and we argued about what to build in the ashes, and I will be honest with you — there were days I wasn't sure we'd get here. But here we are.
>
> I see Larz Rovanky in the back there."

*(She pauses, grins.)*

> "Larz Rovanky, who has not taken a day off since — I believe — the Chelish succession. The man is here. If *Larz* took the day off, I think the rest of us have earned it too."

*(Laughter. Larz, a barrel-chested tanner near the back, turns magnificently red and stares at his boots.)*

> "Welcome to the Swallowtail Festival. Welcome home."

**PC opportunities:**
- Approaching Mayor Deverin after her speech requires moving toward the platform (she is surrounded by well-wishers). Diplomacy DC 6 or simply introduce themselves — she is warm and brief; she will remember them.
- A PC who asks about Larz Rovanky: he is the town tanner, present but deeply uncomfortable. He grunts if spoken to and changes the subject.

---

### Beat 2 — Sheriff Belor Hemlock

Hemlock follows without preamble. He speaks like a man who would rather be on patrol but has accepted this as part of the job.

**Full speech:**

> "Sheriff Hemlock. I'll keep this short.
>
> Tonight there'll be a bonfire. Stay clear of it if you've been drinking. The well buckets will be full and positioned — please leave them where you find them. If you see someone in trouble, don't assume someone else will handle it.
>
> Before I step down — I'd ask for a moment of quiet."

*(He waits. The crowd settles.)*

> "Several of the people who should be standing in this square today are not. I'd like us to remember them."

*(He holds the silence for ten full seconds — long enough to be uncomfortable, which is the point.)*

> "Thank you. Enjoy the festival."

*(He steps off the platform before the applause finishes.)*

**PC opportunities:**
- A PC who catches Hemlock's eye during his speech gets a brief nod — he clocks competent-looking people automatically.
- After the speech, he returns to scanning the crowd perimeter. He will acknowledge a PC who approaches but keeps his attention elsewhere: *"I'm working. Later."* He is not unfriendly — just on duty.

---

### Beat 3 — Lonjiku Kaijitsu (absent) / Cyrdak Drokkus (substitute)

A festival volunteer steps forward and announces that Councillor Lonjiku Kaijitsu sends his regrets — a sudden illness prevents his attendance. The locals in the crowd exchange knowing looks and unsurprised murmurs. Visitors look mildly confused.

Cyrdak Drokkus, owner of the Sandpoint Theater, appears from the side of the platform with an expression of practiced theatricality — hands spread, chin up, the face of a man who has been waiting for exactly this moment.

**Full speech:**

> "A *sudden illness.* Of course. Lonjiku Kaijitsu, struck down on the one day of the year he would absolutely be required to enjoy himself. Remarkable. The gods truly have a sense of comedy."

*(Laughter.)*

> "I'm Cyrdak Drokkus, and I'm going to tell you about this cathedral because I watched them build it, and the story is *extraordinary.*
>
> Year one: the plans arrive from Magnimar. Everyone is very excited. Then we open the plans.
>
> Year two: we argue about the plans.
>
> Year three: we revise the plans, lose the revisions, find the revisions, revise the revisions, and have what I can only describe as a historic town council meeting that I will not describe out of respect for those present. Some of you were there. You know what you did.
>
> Year four: we build the cathedral. It goes up in eight months. Eight! Turns out the arguing was the hard part."

*(He lets the laughter run.)*

> "Father Zantus supervised every stone. Every. Stone. The man has the patience of a saint, which I suppose is a professional requirement.
>
> Tomorrow evening, the Sandpoint Theater opens *The Harpy's Curse* — and I will tell you this: the lead role of Avisera the harpy queen will be played by Allishanda herself. Allishanda! Traveled from Magnimar. In my theater. Come see it or you will regret it for a year."

*(He bows with complete sincerity and departs the platform.)*

**Cyrdak's manner:** Theatrical, warm, quick. He genuinely likes people and is not entirely self-serving — the self-promotion is real but it doesn't crowd out the substance.

**PC opportunities:**
- Any PC who laughs visibly or makes eye contact gets a small acknowledging grin from Cyrdak. He remembers friendly faces.
- A PC who approaches him afterward: he is easy to talk to, pleased to discuss the theater, Allishanda, or the cathedral's construction. He knows most of the town's social gossip and will share it freely if the PC seems interesting.
- A PC who asks about Lonjiku: Cyrdak's tone shifts slightly — still light, but he says *"Interesting man. Keep your expectations low and you won't be disappointed"* and moves on.

---

### Beat 4 — Father Abstalar Zantus

Zantus steps forward last. He is unhurried. He looks at the crowd the way someone looks at something they have been working toward for a long time.

**Full speech:**

> "Thank you. All of you — for being here, for building this, for staying.
>
> This cathedral is not just stone and timber. It is a covenant. With each other, and with something larger than any of us.
>
> Desna asks very little of us. She asks that we move, that we dream, and that we find wonder in the world before we leave it.
>
> Today — let's find some wonder."

*(He steps back from the podium and spreads his arms.)*

> "The Swallowtail Festival is open."

**PC opportunities:**
- After his speech, Zantus is immediately occupied with ceremony logistics — he will speak freely later, but is not available for extended conversation right now.
- A PC with Knowledge (Religion) DC 10 recognizes the covenant language as specifically Desnan — not a generic priestly formula. He means it.

## Scene Constraints (Binding)

- Play each speaker with a distinct voice — Deverin warm, Hemlock spare, Cyrdak theatrical, Zantus sincere
- Lonjiku's absence should feel normal to locals and slightly notable to visitors — do NOT explain or editorialize it
- Do NOT foreshadow the attack during this event; the tone is celebration
- Do NOT introduce unnamed officials, mysterious strangers, or ominous details
