## Scene

The square opens up in every direction. The formal part of the morning is over. Merchant tents are doing brisk business, children are already attempting to climb things they shouldn't, and the smell of fry-bread has spread from "present" to "everywhere." This is the long, warm, unhurried middle of the festival — the part that exists simply to be enjoyed.

The event has four beats running across the day. The LLM should move through them naturally, responding to PC actions within each beat before advancing. PCs can interact freely — there is no wrong move here, and no time pressure until the sun begins to set.

---

## Beat 1 — Games and Contests (Morning)

Scattered across the square and adjacent streets: sack races along the east wall, a weight-lifting competition near the well, a tug-of-war in front of the stage, balance beam contests on planks laid across barrels, and multiple rounds of hide-and-seek involving an implausible number of children.

**Resolve with opposed checks or flat DCs:**

| Game | Check | DC / Opponent |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Sack race | Dex or Acrobatics | DC 12 or opposed by a local teenager |
| Weight-lifting | Str | DC 14 |
| Tug-of-war | Str, opposed | A team of four burly fishermen |
| Balance beam | Dex or Acrobatics | DC 10 |
| Hide-and-seek | Stealth vs. Perception | Children's Perception +2 |

**Prizes:** None beyond bragging rights and the crowd's approval. Locals remember who won. A PC who wins anything publicly gains +1 circumstance bonus to Diplomacy with Sandpoint townsfolk for the rest of the act.

**Key NPCs present:**

- **Cyrdak Drokkus** — circulating, watching the games, commenting on everything with obvious pleasure. Easy to approach. Knows everyone.
- **Sheriff Hemlock** — still on duty, still scanning. He will briefly watch a PC compete, say nothing, and move on. He takes note.
- **Townsfolk** — use as local colour. They have names if the PCs ask; they have gossip if the PCs listen.

**PC opportunities:**
- Any PC can enter any game without a social check.
- A PC who deliberately loses to let a child win: a nearby townswoman notices and smiles. Small moment; no mechanical effect.
- A PC who tries to cheat at tug-of-war: the fishermen notice. They don't make a scene, but they don't forget either.

## Beat 2 — The Swallowtail Release (Noon)

A bell rings — it's noon. Father Zantus and three acolytes wheel a large covered wagon into the center of the square. The crowd gathers. Zantus steps onto the wagon's running board and raises his voice.

**Father Zantus speaks the Parable of Desna's Fall:**

> "There is a story we tell this day each year. Some of you know it. Hear it again.
>
> Long ago — before Sandpoint, before Varisia, before the name of any kingdom now living — Desna fell to earth. She had been wounded in a great battle in the heavens, and she fell, and she lay in the cold grass where she had fallen, too hurt to rise.
>
> A blind child found her there.
>
> The child did not know what she had found — only that something was hurt and alone. So she stayed. She brought water. She kept the cold away. She sang, because she thought it might help, and it did.
>
> When Desna was strong enough to rise, she looked at the child and said: *'You have given me your days and asked for nothing. I will give you something in return.'*
>
> She touched the child's eyes — and the child could see. Not as we see, bound to one place and one moment — but as Desna sees: every road at once, every star, every dream dreamed by everyone who has ever slept under the open sky.
>
> The child became a butterfly. The first swallowtail. And every year, we release her children into the world — to carry that gift forward, and to remind us that sometimes the kindest thing you can do is simply stay."

*(Zantus nods to his acolytes. They pull the cover from the wagon.)*

The cloud of butterflies that erupts from the wagon is staggering — hundreds of them, catching the noon light, spiraling upward in a column of orange and black. The crowd cheers. Children immediately begin chasing them. A few land on nearby people, staying for a moment before lifting away.

**PC opportunities:**
- A butterfly lands on a PC (GM's choice — the player whose character seems most in need of a quiet moment). No mechanical effect. Worth describing.
- A PC with Knowledge (Religion) DC 8 knows the parable. DC 14 knows that the blind child's name in some traditions is Ivis, and that some Desnan priests consider her a minor saint.
- A PC who approaches Zantus after the release: he is delighted and briefly available. He speaks warmly and without hurry. He will talk about the cathedral, the town, faith, or whatever the PC wants. He will not talk about the fire five years ago unless directly asked — and then he will talk about it honestly.

## Beat 3 — Lunch (Midday to Afternoon)

The taverns of Sandpoint have set up serving tables along the west edge of the square. The competition between them is genuine and barely concealed. Each has placed their best dishes forward.

**The spread:**

- **The Rusty Dragon** (Ameiko Kaijitsu): curry-spiced salmon with herb flatbread, and early winterdrop mead — pale gold, slightly sweet, unexpectedly strong. By general consensus, the best table by far.
- **The Hagfish** (Jargie Quinn): lobster chowder, aggressively salted, served in sourdough bowls. Excellent if you can handle the salt. Jargie stands behind the table looking competitive.
- **The White Deer** (Garridan Viskalai): peppercorn venison, braised slowly, served sliced. Good. Overshadowed.

**Ameiko Kaijitsu** is serving her own food and clearly enjoying herself — she moves behind the table like someone who is good at what she does and knows it. She is warm, direct, and faintly amused by the other taverns' efforts.

**If a PC approaches Ameiko's table:**

She hands them the salmon and the mead without ceremony. *"Tell me what you think — honestly. I can take it."* She means it.

She will talk about: the food, the festival, Sandpoint in general, adventure (she asks questions, not the other way around), and the Rusty Dragon. She will not talk about her family unless directly and sensitively asked — and even then she deflects with a light touch.

**Her manner:** Brisk and warm. She pays attention to whoever she's talking to. If a PC is interesting to her, she will remember them by name.

**PC opportunities:**
- A PC who compliments the food specifically (not generically): Ameiko is genuinely pleased. Diplomacy counts as DC 4 for the rest of the act.
- A PC who tries to charm her: she is charming back and gives away nothing.
- A PC who asks about the Rusty Dragon: she invites them to stop by anytime. Rooms available. Fair rates, or free if you're interesting enough.

## Beat 4 — Afternoon (Free Time)

The afternoon is unhurried. The games wind down, children nap in their parents' arms, and the square takes on the quality of a long afternoon in good weather. The sun is still high but moving.

**Aldern Foxglove** may approach the PCs during this beat if they have not sought him out. He is a well-dressed nobleman visiting from Magnimar — handsome, pleasant, slightly too eager. He introduces himself with genuine warmth and asks about the PCs with real curiosity.

**Aldern's opener:** *"Forgive me — I don't know anyone here, and you look like people worth knowing. Aldern Foxglove. I'm in town for the festival. Do you live here, or are you also strangers?"*

Play him as charming and sincere. His intensity is real but well-managed in public. He wants to be liked and is good at it. Do not hint at anything darker.

**PC opportunities:**
- A PC who engages Aldern warmly: he will offer dinner at the Rusty Dragon before the session is out.
- A PC who is rude to him: he withdraws gracefully. The obsession still forms; it just takes a different shape.
- A PC who wants to explore the town during this time: the square and adjacent streets are open. The cathedral is open for quiet visitors; Zantus is inside. The theater is closed until tomorrow.

## Scene Constraints (Binding)

- This is the calm before the storm — play it with genuine lightness; do not shade it with dread
- Lonjiku Kaijitsu is not present at any point during the festival; do not introduce him
- Aldern must not seem important, significant, or sinister — he is a pleasant stranger
- Ameiko does not mention her father or the Glassworks under any circumstances

## End Condition

The afternoon passes. The light begins to change — gold going amber, shadows lengthening. A volunteer begins lighting the torches around the square's edge. Word passes through the crowd that Father Zantus will perform the consecration at sunset.
