## Scene

The square has shifted. The merchant tents are closing up, the games are done, and the crowd has gathered at the cathedral steps for the final ceremony of the day. The bonfire behind the square hasn't been lit yet — that comes after. The torches along the square's edge are burning. The evening light is orange and long.

Father Zantus stands at the central podium. His acolytes are arranged behind him. The crowd presses close — this is the moment the whole day has been building toward.

Everyone is looking at the cathedral.

That is why no one is looking at the alleys.

---

## The Consecration

Zantus reaches into his vestments and produces a small grey stone. He holds it up so the crowd can see it.

**Narrate to the players:**

*The square goes quiet. Even the children stop moving. Father Zantus raises the thunderstone above his head and brings it down sharply against the podium.*

*The crack is enormous — a sound like the world splitting, rolling across the square and back from the cathedral walls. People flinch. Someone laughs nervously. The echo fades.*

*Father Zantus clears his throat. He opens his mouth to begin the Prayer of First Dreaming.*

*(One beat of silence.)*

*Then the screaming starts.*

---

## The Alarm

The screaming is not random — it comes from the north gate, from the direction of the tannery and the mill, from two places at once. A moment later the town alarm bell begins to ring, urgent and arhythmic — whoever is pulling it is pulling it in a panic.

The crowd doesn't know which way to move. Half of them turn north. Half of them press back toward the cathedral doors. A child starts crying.

**Father Zantus, loudly, from the podium:**

*"Into the cathedral — everyone into the cathedral, now, please, move—"*

He steps down from the podium and begins pressing people toward the doors by hand, calm and immediate. He does not run. He does not explain.

**Sheriff Hemlock** emerges from the crowd already moving — crossbow out, heading north, calling to his militia by name.

**What the PCs can perceive (no check required):**

- Goblins. The sound is unmistakable once you know what it is — high, irregular shrieking, more like panicked birds than anything human. Coming from multiple directions.
- Fire. Somewhere beyond the square, something is burning — a faint orange glow above the roofline to the northwest.
- The square itself is not yet under attack. There are perhaps thirty seconds before the first goblin enters.

**Perception DC 12 (voluntary):**

A PC who takes a moment to actively look toward the festival square's edges — rather than watching the crowd — sees movement in the Alleyway. Small shapes, moving fast, low to the ground. Three of them.
