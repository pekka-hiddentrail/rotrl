## Scene

The square reeks of smoke. One market stall is still burning — militia are throwing buckets of well water at the base of the flames with the grim efficiency of people who have done this before. The warchanter's body lies where she fell. Somewhere behind the Well, a dog is barking without stopping.

Sheriff Hemlock strides across the square from the direction of the garrison. He has a crossbow across his back and blood on his knuckles — not his own. Four militia follow him in loose formation. He scans the square once, counts the dead, then looks at the PCs.

This pause is shorter than the last. Something is still moving in the Alleyway — the sound of claws on cobblestone.

## NPCs Present

### Sheriff Hemlock — Center (moving)

Hemlock is not here to chat. He takes in the scene in three seconds and reaches a conclusion: these people know what they're doing.

**What he offers without being asked:** A brief, direct assessment: *"Good work. More coming — I can hear them."* He does not elaborate. He positions two militia at the Alleyway entrance and steps back to give the PCs room.

**If a PC speaks to him:** He answers in two sentences or fewer. He will confirm that more goblins are coming, that the cathedral is secured, and that his militia are handling civilians. He does not speculate on why the attack is happening.

**If a PC asks him to help fight:** *"My people are keeping the square clear. You've got the goblins."* He means it as a compliment. He does not join the fight directly — he is managing the perimeter.

**His manner:** Clipped, professional, zero wasted words. A nod to a PC who performed well carries more weight than a speech would.

### Aldern Foxglove — Well

Shaken but upright. If the PCs glance toward the Well, they can see him trying not to look as frightened as he is. He raises a hand in a small, uncertain wave if anyone looks at him.

He does not speak unless a PC approaches. If they do, he says: *"I'm fine. Brion's fine."* A beat. *"Are you all right?"* He is genuinely asking.
