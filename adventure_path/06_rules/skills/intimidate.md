# Intimidate

**Triggers:** intimidate, intimidating, threaten, threatening, coerce, coercing, frighten, scare, demoralize, bully, menace, demand

## Influencing a Target (Out of Combat)

**DC:** 10 + target's Hit Dice + target's Wisdom modifier

Success: target acts friendly toward you for 1d6 × 10 minutes — gives information, takes non-dangerous actions, offers limited assistance.
Fail by 5+: target attempts to deceive you or hinder your activities.
After the effect expires, target treats you as Unfriendly and may report you to authorities.

## Demoralize (Combat)

**DC:** 10 + target's Hit Dice + target's Wisdom modifier

Success: target is Shaken for 1 round. Duration increases by 1 round for every 5 by which you beat the DC.
Target must be within 30 feet and able to clearly see and hear you.
Repeated demoralize attempts only extend duration; they do not escalate the fear condition beyond Shaken.

## GM Notes

- Intimidation leaves emotional residue — NPCs who are coerced do not forget it
- Using Intimidate on an NPC the party wants as a long-term ally is almost always a mistake
- In high-stakes social situations (courts, public spaces), attempting Intimidate may have political consequences regardless of success
- Out of combat, Intimidate can force short-term compliance, but it does not create lasting goodwill

## What It Does

Intimidate is used to frighten opponents or get them to act in a way that benefits you through verbal threats and displays of prowess. Unlike Diplomacy, it forces compliance through fear and usually leaves resentment behind.

## Action

Influencing a target's attitude: 1 minute of conversation.
Demoralizing an opponent: standard action.

## Try Again

You can attempt to Intimidate an opponent again, but each additional check increases the DC by +5. This resets after 1 hour.

## Special

+4 bonus if you are larger than your target; −4 penalty if you are smaller.
If you have the Persuasive feat, you get a bonus on Intimidate checks.
A half-orc gets a +2 bonus on Intimidate checks.
