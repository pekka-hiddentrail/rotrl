# Heal

**Triggers:** heal, healing, treat wounds, stabilize, first aid, treat poison, treat disease, bandage, long-term care, healer, tend wounds, patch up, revive, stop bleeding, dress wounds, bind wounds, field medicine

## Resolution

**DC check using Wisdom modifier + Heal ranks.**

| Task | DC | Action |
|---|---|---|
| First Aid (stabilize) | 15 | Standard |
| Treat wounds from caltrops, spike growth, or spike stones | 15 | Standard or 10 min |
| Long-term care | 15 | 8 hrs rest |
| Treat Deadly Wounds | 20 | 1 hour |
| Treat Poison | Poison's save DC | Standard |
| Treat Disease | Disease's save DC | 10 minutes |

**First Aid:** Stabilizes a dying character (negative HP, losing HP per round/hour/day). Does not restore HP. Also ends bleed damage.

**Treat Deadly Wounds:** Restores **1 HP per level** of the creature. Exceed DC by 5+: add your Wisdom modifier (if positive). Requires **two healer's kit uses** (−2 penalty per missing use). Patient cannot benefit more than once per day and only within 24 hours of being injured.

**Long-term care:** Patient recovers at twice normal rate: 2 HP/level per 8-hr rest, or 4 HP/level per full day of complete rest. Also applies to ability damage. Up to 6 patients at once. **Cannot treat yourself.**

**Treat Poison / Treat Disease:** Make a Heal check each time the patient makes a saving throw vs the effect. Beat the poison's or disease's save DC: patient gains **+4 competence bonus** on that saving throw.

**Healer's kit:** +2 circumstance bonus; 10 uses. Two uses consumed per Treat Deadly Wounds. −2 penalty per missing use.

## GM Notes

- **Treat Deadly Wounds is 1 HP/level, not HP = check result** — common misread. On a great roll (beat DC by 5+ with Wis +3), the Wisdom modifier adds meaningfully. The once-per-day limit per patient is strict.
- **First Aid does not restore HP** — it stops the −1/round bleed and ends any ongoing bleed damage. Be explicit when players ask what happened after the check.
- **Two kit uses per Treat Deadly Wounds** — a standard 10-use kit supports only 5 applications. Buying a second kit before Thistletop is wise.
- **Long-term care cannot be self-applied** — the healer must be a different character. Critical if the party's only healer is the one badly hurt.
- **Nualia's corruption** — ghoul fever and similar blights become relevant in the Catacombs and at Thistletop. Treat Disease must be re-applied each time the patient saves; remind the player of this.
- **Caltrops at Thistletop** — goblins scatter caltrops in approach corridors. DC 15 Heal removes the half-speed penalty as a standard action; call for the roll right after combat ends.

## What It Does

Heal represents training in medicine, first aid, and long-term patient care. It lets you stabilize dying characters, end bleed effects, remove movement penalties from caltrops and similar hazards, restore hit points without magic, and help patients fight off poisons and diseases.

## Untrained

Can be attempted untrained. First Aid, caltrops treatment, and long-term care (all DC 15) are available to anyone. Treat Deadly Wounds (DC 20) can technically be attempted untrained. Treat Poison and Treat Disease require beating the poison's or disease's own save DC, which varies.

## Action

Varies by task:
- **First Aid, Treat Poison, treat caltrop wounds:** Standard action.
- **Treat Disease, treat spike growth / spike stones wounds:** 10 minutes.
- **Treat Deadly Wounds:** 1 hour.
- **Long-term care:** 8 hours of light activity.

## Try Again

Varies. Generally you cannot retry a Heal check without witnessing proof that the original attempt failed. Exception: you can always retry First Aid as long as the target is still alive.

## Special

- A character with the **Self-Sufficient** feat gains a bonus on Heal checks.
- A **healer's kit** grants +2 circumstance bonus on all Heal checks (10 uses total; two uses consumed per Treat Deadly Wounds).
