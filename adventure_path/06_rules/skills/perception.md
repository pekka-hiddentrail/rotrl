# Perception

**Triggers:** perception, look around, look at, look for, search, searching, examine, examining, inspect, notice, spot, listen, hear, hearing, scan, keep watch, watch for, do i see, do i notice, check for traps, check for, peer, peer at, peer into, investigate

## What It Does

Perception covers all five senses, including sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell. It is used both for opposed checks (most commonly versus Stealth) and for noticing details in the environment.

## Opposed Check

The most common Perception use is an opposed check versus an opponent's Stealth check to notice the opponent and avoid being surprised.

If you succeed, you notice the opponent and can react accordingly. If you fail, your opponent may sneak past you, attack you, or otherwise act without being noticed.

## Perception Modifiers

| Condition | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Distance to the source, object, or creature | +1 DC per 10 feet |
| Through a closed door | +5 DC |
| Through a wall | +10 DC per foot of thickness |
| Favorable conditions | -2 DC |
| Unfavorable conditions | +2 DC |
| Terrible conditions | +5 DC |
| Creature making the check is distracted | +5 DC |
| Creature making the check is asleep | +10 DC |
| Creature or object is invisible | +20 DC |

Favorable and unfavorable conditions depend on the sense being used. For example, bright light can lower sight-based DCs, while low light can raise them. Background noise can raise hearing DCs, and competing odors can raise scent DCs. Terrible conditions are more extreme versions of unfavorable conditions.

## Action

Most Perception checks are reactive, made in response to an observable stimulus.

Intentionally searching for a stimulus is a move action.

## Try Again

Yes. You can try to sense something you missed the first time, as long as the stimulus is still present.

## Special

Elves, half-elves, gnomes, and halflings receive a +2 racial bonus on Perception checks.

Creatures with the scent special quality have a +8 bonus on Perception checks made to detect a scent.

Creatures with the tremorsense special quality have a +8 bonus on Perception checks against creatures touching the ground and automatically make such checks within their tremorsense range.

A spellcaster with a hawk or owl familiar gains a +3 bonus on Perception checks.

If you have the Alertness feat, you get a bonus on Perception checks.

## GM Notes

- Most important first call: decide whether this is opposed (usually vs Stealth) or a fixed detail DC
- Apply environmental modifiers to the DC based on sense conditions and distance
- Keep secret information secret: tell players what they notice, not what they failed to notice
