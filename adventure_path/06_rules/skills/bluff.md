# Bluff

**Triggers:** bluff, bluffing, lie, lying, deceive, deceiving, deception, trick, tricking, mislead, misleading, fast talk, feint, create a distraction, pretend, fabricate, fool, fooling, convince, convincing, make believe, make her believe, make him believe, make them believe, act as if, act like, pass off, pass as, impersonate, claim falsely, deny falsely

## Resolution

**Opposed check: Bluff vs target's Sense Motive.**

Lie difficulty modifiers applied to the Bluff roll:

| Circumstances | Bluff Modifier |
|---|---:|
| Target wants to believe you | +5 |
| The lie is believable | +0 |
| The lie is unlikely | −5 |
| The lie is far-fetched | −10 |
| The lie is impossible | −20 |
| Target is drunk or impaired | +5 |
| You possess convincing proof | up to +10 |

**Success:** Target believes the statement for now; may reconsider if new evidence surfaces.
**Failure:** Target knows or strongly suspects the lie. Attitude may worsen.
**Failure by 5+:** Target is certain of deception. Trust damage occurs.

## Feint in Combat

DC = 10 + opponent's BAB + opponent's Wisdom mod.
If opponent is trained in Sense Motive: DC = 10 + Sense Motive bonus (whichever is higher).
Success: opponent denied Dexterity bonus to AC against your next attack.

## GM Notes

- Bluff does not erase memory — a successful bluff means the NPC believes it now, not forever
- Very intelligent or perceptive NPCs (high Sense Motive) will be harder to fool even on plausible lies
- Consider whether an NPC would have reason to be on guard (has the party deceived them before?)
- Some lies are so improbable it is impossible to convince anyone they are true, regardless of the roll

## What It Does

Bluff creates a false belief in a target's mind through a lie, a feint in combat, or a secret message. It is primarily resolved as an opposed check against the target's Sense Motive.

## Secret Messages

You can use Bluff to pass hidden messages to another character without others understanding your true meaning. DC 15 for simple messages, DC 20 for complex. On success the target automatically understands you. Fail by 5+: you deliver the wrong message. Others can decipher the message by succeeding at an opposed Sense Motive check against your Bluff result.

## Action

Deceiving someone takes at least 1 round (longer for elaborate lies, GM discretion).
Feinting in combat is a standard action.
Delivering a secret message takes twice as long as normal.

## Try Again

If you fail to deceive someone, further attempts are at −10 and may be impossible (GM discretion).
You can attempt to feint again if you fail.
Secret messages can be relayed again if the first attempt fails.

## Special

A spellcaster with a viper familiar gains a +3 bonus on Bluff checks.
If you have the Deceitful feat, you get a bonus on Bluff checks.
