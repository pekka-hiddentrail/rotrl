# Diplomacy

**Triggers:** diplomacy, persuade, persuading, convince, convincing, negotiate, negotiating, appeal, request, make a case, talk them into, reason with, plead

## What It Does

Diplomacy is used to persuade others to agree with your arguments, resolve differences, gather valuable information or rumors, and negotiate conflicts using proper etiquette and manners. In play, it most often changes an NPC's attitude, secures a request from a creature that is at least indifferent, or gathers information over time.

## Attitude Scale

Hostile → Unfriendly → Indifferent → Friendly → Helpful

You usually begin by determining the creature's starting attitude. With a successful Diplomacy check, that attitude improves by one step. For every 5 by which your result exceeds the DC, the creature's attitude improves by one additional step, up to a maximum of two steps by this rule unless the GM overrides it.

If you fail by 4 or less, the attitude does not change. If you fail by 5 or more, the creature's attitude decreases by one step.

You cannot use Diplomacy against a creature that does not understand you or has an Intelligence of 3 or less.

Diplomacy is generally ineffective in combat and against creatures that intend to harm you or your allies in the immediate future.

Any attitude shift caused through Diplomacy generally lasts for 1d4 hours, though the duration can be much shorter or much longer depending on the situation.

## Attitude Shift DCs

| Starting Attitude | DC to Improve by One Step |
|---|---|
| Hostile | 25 + creature's Charisma modifier |
| Unfriendly | 20 + creature's Charisma modifier |
| Indifferent | 15 + creature's Charisma modifier |
| Friendly | 10 + creature's Charisma modifier |
| Helpful | 0 + creature's Charisma modifier |

## Requests

If a creature's attitude toward you is at least indifferent, you can make requests of the creature. This is an additional Diplomacy check, using the creature's current attitude to determine the base DC, with the following request modifiers.

| Request | Modifier |
|---|---:|
| Give simple advice or directions | -5 |
| Give detailed advice | +0 |
| Give simple aid | +0 |
| Reveal an unimportant secret | +5 |
| Give lengthy or complicated aid | +5 |
| Give dangerous aid | +10 |
| Reveal an important secret | +10 or more |
| Give aid that could result in punishment | +15 or more |
| Additional requests | +5 per request |

Once a creature's attitude has shifted to helpful, it gives in to most requests without a check unless the request is against its nature or puts it in serious peril. Some requests automatically fail if they go against the creature's values or nature, subject to GM discretion.

## Gather Information

You can use Diplomacy to gather information about a specific topic or individual. To do this, you must spend at least 1d4 hours canvassing people at local taverns, markets, and gathering places.

The DC depends on the obscurity of the information sought, but for most commonly known facts or rumors it is 10. For obscure or secret knowledge, the DC might increase to 20 or higher. Some topics may simply be unknown to common folk.

## Action

Using Diplomacy to influence a creature's attitude takes 1 minute of continuous interaction.

Making a request of a creature takes 1 or more rounds of interaction, depending on the complexity of the request.

Using Diplomacy to gather information takes 1d4 hours of work searching for rumors and informants.

## Try Again

You cannot use Diplomacy to influence a given creature's attitude more than once in a 24-hour period.

If a request is refused, the result does not change with additional checks, although other requests might still be made.

You can retry Diplomacy checks made to gather information.

## Special

If you have the Persuasive feat, you gain a bonus on Diplomacy checks.

## GM Notes

- Diplomacy cannot make an NPC do something self-destructive or against their core principles regardless of the roll
- A natural 20 does not guarantee success on an impossible request
- Track attitude shifts between sessions — NPCs remember how they were treated
- For ordinary Diplomacy, the key input is the target's starting attitude and Charisma modifier
- Treat attitude shifts and requests as separate checks when the rules call for both
