# Knowledge (History)

**Triggers:** knowledge history, know history, historical knowledge, what happened here, ancient history, old history, who built this, when was this built, what was this place, origins of, history of this place, ancient empire, thassilon, thassilonian, ancient rune, rune meaning, sihedron, sihedron rune, what do i know about this structure, ancient ruins, ruins, old ruins, runelord, runelords, what era, what age, what period

## Resolution

**DC check using INT modifier + Knowledge (History) ranks.** Untrained characters (0 ranks) can attempt DC 10 checks only.

| DC | Information Type |
|---|---|
| 10 | Know a recent or historically significant event; broad strokes of Golarion’s recorded history |
| 15 | Determine the approximate date of a specific event; Thassilon existed, the Sihedron Rune is Thassilonian |
| 20 | Know an obscure or ancient historical event; Thassilon’s seven sin-domains, each Runelord’s school |
| 25 | Identify specific Runelords by name and domain (Alaznist — Wrath; Karzoug — Greed; etc.) |
| 30 | Specific Thassilonian construction techniques, dates, the empire’s fall, or function of a specific structure |

## GM Notes

- History is the primary skill for interpreting Thassilonian ruins, runes, and structures
- Brodert Quink (town sage) has a much higher effective bonus than any PC — directing PCs to him is valid if they fail
- First Act I exposure: the Swallowtail Festival takes place at the new cathedral, built on the site of the old chapel fire — DC 15 to know the chapel stood on older foundations
- The Sihedron Rune appearing at a murder scene (Act II) should immediately prompt a History check

## What It Does

Knowledge (History) covers wars, colonies, migrations, founding of cities, and the history of the world, a nation, or a people. In this campaign it is the primary tool for interpreting the ruins and artefacts of the ancient empire of Thassilon.

## Untrained

Can be attempted untrained for DC 10 or lower. Access to a historical library removes the DC 10 limit, but check time increases to 1d4 hours. A particularly complete library may grant a bonus on the check.

## Action

Usually no action required to recall information.

## Try Again

No. Once you fail a Knowledge check, you simply don't recall the information.

## Special

A bard adds half her class level (minimum 1) on all Knowledge checks, even untrained.
Bardic Knowledge covers History natively.
