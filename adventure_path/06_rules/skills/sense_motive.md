# Sense Motive

**Triggers:** sense motive, sense their motive, read them, read their, detect lie, detect lying, is he lying, is she lying, are they lying, can i tell, something seems off, seems suspicious, get a feeling, gut feeling, study their face, watch their reaction

## What It Does

Sense Motive lets you detect falsehoods and true intentions. It is most often used to avoid being bluffed, but it also supports social hunches, detecting enchantment influence, and discerning secret messages.

## Check

A successful Sense Motive check lets you avoid being bluffed (see Bluff).

You can also use this skill to determine when "something is up" or to assess someone's trustworthiness.

## Task DCs

| Task | Sense Motive DC |
|---|---|
| Hunch | 20 |
| Sense enchantment | 25 or 15 |
| Discern secret message | Varies |

## Hunch

This use is a gut assessment of a social situation. You might get the feeling from another's behavior that something is wrong, such as when speaking to an impostor. You might also get the feeling that someone is trustworthy.

## Sense Enchantment

You can tell that someone's behavior is being influenced by an enchantment effect even if that person is not aware of it.

The usual DC is 25, but if the target is dominated, the DC is 15 because of the limited range of the target's activities.

## Discern Secret Message

You can use Sense Motive to detect that a hidden message is being transmitted via Bluff.

In this case, your Sense Motive check is opposed by the Bluff check of the character transmitting the message.

For each piece of information relating to the message that you are missing, you take a -2 penalty on your Sense Motive check.

Result bands:

- Success by 4 or less: you know that something hidden is being communicated, but you learn nothing specific.
- Success by 5 or more: you intercept and understand the message.
- Failure by 4 or less: you do not detect hidden communication.
- Failure by 5 or more: you might infer false information.

## Action

Trying to gain information with Sense Motive generally takes at least 1 minute, and you could spend a whole evening trying to get a sense of the people around you.

## Try Again

No, though you may make a Sense Motive check for each Bluff check made against you.

## Special

A ranger gains a bonus on Sense Motive checks when using this skill against a favored enemy.

If you have the Alertness feat, you get a bonus on Sense Motive checks.

## GM Notes

- Resolve normal lying as Sense Motive opposed by Bluff
- Keep secret-message outcomes by margin (<=4 or >=5) explicit, because outcome quality depends on degree of success/failure
- On failed checks, avoid overcommitting to certainty; return uncertainty unless the rules call for false inference
