# Sense Motive

**Triggers:** sense motive, sense their motive, read them, read their, detect lie, detect lying, is he lying, is she lying, are they lying, can i tell, something seems off, seems suspicious, get a feeling, gut feeling, study their face, watch their reaction

## Resolution

**Opposed check vs Bluff** when resisting a lie or detecting a feint.
For other uses, fixed DCs:

| Task | DC |
|---|---|
| Hunch (gut read of a social situation) | 20 |
| Sense enchantment influence | 25 (or 15 if target is dominated) |
| Discern secret message | Opposed by Bluff |

## Secret Message Detection (by margin)

- Success by 4 or less: you know something hidden is being communicated, but learn nothing specific.
- Success by 5+: you intercept and understand the message.
- Failure by 4 or less: you detect nothing.
- Failure by 5+: you may infer false information.

## GM Notes

- Resolve normal lying as Sense Motive opposed by Bluff
- Keep secret-message outcomes by margin explicit — quality of result depends on degree of success or failure
- On failed checks, avoid overcommitting to certainty; return uncertainty unless the rules call for false inference

## What It Does

Sense Motive lets you detect falsehoods and true intentions. It is most often used to avoid being bluffed, but also supports social hunches, detecting enchantment influence, and discerning secret messages.

## Action

Gaining information with Sense Motive generally takes at least 1 minute. You could spend a whole evening trying to get a read on those around you.

## Try Again

No, though you may make a Sense Motive check for each Bluff check made against you.

## Special

A ranger gains a bonus on Sense Motive checks against a favored enemy.
If you have the Alertness feat, you get a bonus on Sense Motive checks.
